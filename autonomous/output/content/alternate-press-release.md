# ALTERNATE: New Zealand Creators Release One of the World's First Fully AI-Generated Narrative Films

**Auckland, New Zealand — February 14, 2026**

Three New Zealand creators have released ALTERNATE, a 15-minute science fiction film made entirely using artificial intelligence — believed to be among the first fully AI-generated narrative films in the world.

The film follows three friends who, during an altered state of consciousness, use AI to generate visions of their alternate lives — the paths they didn't take. What begins as an experiment spirals into an existential journey through cyberpunk cityscapes, untouched jungle paradises, and medieval battlefields, ultimately forcing them to confront whether the life they have is the one they would choose.

### The Meta Layer

ALTERNATE was created by Tom Hall-Taylor, Zac Udy, and Trent Walker over seven days using a suite of AI tools including Claude (Anthropic), Higgsfield, Kling 3.0, and Nano Banana Pro. The total production cost was approximately $50 USD in API fees — with no cameras, no actors, and no traditional film equipment.

"We were genuinely in an altered state while making a film about being in an altered state," said Hall-Taylor. "We used AI to create a story about AI showing us alternate realities. The meta layers are intentional — and a bit dizzying."

### A New Creative Medium

The creators position ALTERNATE not as a replacement for human filmmaking, but as proof of concept for a new creative medium.

"This isn't about replacing human storytellers," said Udy, who brings a background in creative production. "It's about what's now possible for anyone with a vision and $50. The barriers to cinematic storytelling just collapsed."
