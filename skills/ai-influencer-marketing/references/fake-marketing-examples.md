# Smart Fake Marketing Examples

## The Psychology

People believe what looks organic. Polished ads = ignored. "Caught in the wild" = shared.

---

## Tactic 1: Fake Billboard Placements

**What:** Generate movie poster/billboard in famous locations

**Prompts:**
```
ALTERNATE movie poster on massive billboard in Times Square New York, night time, neon lights reflecting, crowds walking past, shot from street level with phone camera quality, slight motion blur
```

```
ALTERNATE movie poster on London Underground platform, commuters waiting, fluorescent lighting, candid phone photo aesthetic
```

```
Giant ALTERNATE billboard on Shibuya crossing Tokyo, rainy night, umbrella crowds, neon reflections on wet street
```

**Make believable:**
- Add slight blur/grain
- Include partial obstructions (heads, objects)
- Off-center framing
- "Accidentally" captured feel

## Rules for Believability

1. **Never too polished** — Real viral content has imperfections
2. **Context > Content** — Show the situation, not just the thing
3. **Native to platform** — Match how real users post
4. **Partial information** — Don't explain everything, create curiosity
5. **Emotional truth** — Even if fake, the reaction should feel real

---

## What NOT to Do

❌ Perfect lighting/composition (screams "ad")
❌ Obvious watermarks or branding
❌ Overproduced captions
❌ Claims that could be legally problematic
❌ Fake celebrity likeness without permission
