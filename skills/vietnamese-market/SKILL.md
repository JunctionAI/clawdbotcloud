# Vietnamese Market - Health Supplements & Video Marketing

## Cultural Context

### Health Philosophy
Vietnamese consumers blend:
- **Traditional Eastern medicine** (thuốc nam, thuốc bắc)
- **Modern Western supplements**
- **Strong family-centered health decisions**

Health is a **family matter** — purchases often made for parents/grandparents by children.

### Trust Factors (Critical)
1. **Country of origin matters enormously**
   - New Zealand = "clean, green, pure" (very positive)
   - Australia = trusted
   - USA = mixed (good for some categories)
   - China = low trust for health products

2. **Certifications and quality marks**
   - Laboratory tested
   - HACCP, GMP
   - "Made in New Zealand" is a selling point

3. **Word of mouth is king**
   - Family recommendations
   - Community endorsements
   - Real testimonials from Vietnamese users

### Health Concerns (Priorities)

**For Elderly (Targeting with DBH):**
1. Joint health — very high concern
2. Bone health / Calcium — especially women
3. Heart & circulation
4. Memory / cognitive decline
5. General vitality and energy

**For Middle-Aged Women:**
1. Menopause symptoms
2. Bone density / osteoporosis fear
3. Fatigue / energy
4. Skin health / anti-aging

**Cultural Health Beliefs:**
- "Bổ máu" (blood nourishment) — highly valued concept
- Animal-derived supplements respected (deer, shark, sea cucumber)
- Warmth/cold balance in body
- Prevention over cure mentality

## Video Marketing for Vietnamese Audiences

### What Works

**Format Preferences:**
- **Testimonial-style** — real people sharing experiences
- **Educational** — explain the science simply
- **Before/after narratives** — "I used to feel X, now I feel Y"
- **Family context** — show multi-generational care

**Visual Style:**
- Clean, professional look (not overly flashy)
- Product clearly visible
- Show the country of origin (NZ scenery, flags)
- Happy, healthy-looking people (not models — real people)

**Length:**
- Social media: 30-60 seconds (hooks in first 3 seconds)
- YouTube/educational: 3-5 minutes acceptable
- Facebook native performs well in Vietnamese community

### Language Considerations

**If speaking English (for NZ Vietnamese):**
- Speak clearly, not too fast
- Use simple words
- Visual text overlays in Vietnamese help
- Subtitles highly recommended

**Key Vietnamese Terms to Know:**
- Thuốc bổ = Health supplement
- Bổ máu = Blood nourishment (perfect for Deer Blood)
- Canxi = Calcium
- Xương = Bones
- Trí nhớ = Memory
- Tuổi mãn kinh = Menopause

**Tone:**
- Respectful, not aggressive sales
- Educational, informative
- Caring, family-oriented
- Authentic testimonials over polished ads

### Platform Strategy

**Facebook:**
- #1 platform for Vietnamese diaspora
- Groups are very active (community-based)
- Native video gets good reach
- Comment engagement matters

**YouTube:**
- Growing, especially for education
- Longer form content works
- Search-friendly for health topics

**TikTok:**
- Younger Vietnamese audience
- Less relevant for health supplements targeting elderly
- Could work for "gifting to parents" angle

### Trust-Building Content Ideas

1. **Factory/Lab Tour** — show clean NZ manufacturing
2. **Ingredient Sourcing** — NZ deer farms, natural setting
3. **Real Customer Stories** — Vietnamese customers in NZ
4. **Expert Endorsement** — pharmacist or doctor comment
5. **Family Gift Story** — "I bought this for my mother..."

## Filming Tips

### Setting
- Clean, bright, professional
- Subtle NZ elements (fern, landscape backdrop)
- Product prominently displayed

### Presenter
- Warm, approachable demeanor
- Look directly at camera
- Genuine enthusiasm, not scripted sales
- If possible, have Vietnamese translator/subtitles

### Script Structure
1. **Hook (5 sec):** Problem or benefit statement
2. **Problem (15 sec):** What they're experiencing
3. **Solution (30 sec):** How product helps, simple science
4. **Proof (15 sec):** Testimonial, certification, origin
5. **Call to Action (10 sec):** Where to buy, special offer

### Production Notes
- Good audio is MORE important than perfect video
- Natural lighting preferred
- Show product being held, used
- B-roll of NZ nature/farms adds credibility
