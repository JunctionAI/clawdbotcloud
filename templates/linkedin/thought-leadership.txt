LinkedIn Thought Leadership Template
===================================

[HOOK - First line is everything]

Make it personal, surprising, or valuable.
No "I'm excited to share..." or "I'm thrilled to announce..."

Examples:
- "I lost $50k on my first business. Here's what I learned:"
- "Your marketing strategy is backwards. Here's why:"
- "After 10 years in [industry], this is what nobody tells you:"

[Expand the hook]

Add context. Why does this matter?
What made you write this post?

[Main content - Story or insights]

Option A - Story Arc:
- Set the scene
- The challenge/conflict
- What you tried
- What worked (or didn't)
- The lesson

Option B - Framework/List:
- Point 1 with example
- Point 2 with example
- Point 3 with example

Keep paragraphs SHORT.
2-3 sentences max.

Use line breaks generously.

[The takeaway]

What's the key lesson?
How can they apply it?

[Call to action]

Examples:
- "What's your take on this? Drop a comment below."
- "If you found this valuable, repost it for your network."
- "Want more insights like this? Follow me for weekly posts on [topic]."
- "Struggling with [problem]? DM me, happy to share what worked for me."

LINKEDIN BEST PRACTICES:

✅ DO:
- Hook in first line (shows before "see more")
- Personal stories over corporate speak
- Short paragraphs (mobile-friendly)
- Include a CTA
- 3-5 hashtags (research popular ones in your niche)
- Engage with comments within first hour
- Post between 8-10am on weekdays

❌ DON'T:
- Start with "I'm excited to announce..."
- Include links in the post (kills reach)
- Make it about you (make it about them)
- Be too salesy
- Ignore comments

ENGAGEMENT BOOSTERS:
- Ask questions
- Share vulnerable moments
- Challenge conventional wisdom
- Use "You" not "I"
- Reply to every comment
