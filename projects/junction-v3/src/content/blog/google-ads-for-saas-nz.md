# Google Ads for SaaS Companies in NZ: The Complete Guide to Driving Demos and Free Trials

SaaS businesses have a unique advantage with Google Ads: your product can be purchased, trialled, or demoed entirely online. There&apos;s no need for a sales team to close every deal from scratch — a well-structured Google Ads campaign can drive someone from searching a problem to starting a free trial in minutes.

But SaaS Google Ads also has unique challenges. The sales cycle is longer than ecommerce. Conversions are softer (a trial signup is not revenue — it&apos;s the beginning of a funnel). And for NZ SaaS companies, you&apos;re often competing against well-funded international players with significantly larger ad budgets.

This guide explains how to run Google Ads campaigns that work for NZ SaaS businesses.

---

## Defining Success: What Are You Optimising For?

Before running a single ad, define your conversion goal. For SaaS, the options are:

**Free trial signup**: The user creates an account and starts a free trial. High volume, lower intent — some will never become paying customers.

**Demo request**: The user fills out a form requesting a product demonstration. Lower volume, higher intent — someone who requests a demo is genuinely evaluating your product.

**Freemium signup**: Similar to free trial but permanent. Works well for product-led growth models.

**Direct purchase**: If your product is self-serve and low-price, direct purchase from an ad is viable.

For most NZ B2B SaaS companies, demo requests or free trial signups are the primary goals. Align your campaigns, bidding strategy, and landing pages around the specific goal that best maps to revenue.

## Keyword Strategy for SaaS Google Ads

SaaS keywords fall into several categories, each requiring a different approach.

### Problem/Pain Keywords

These target users experiencing the problem your software solves, before they&apos;re searching for software specifically.

Examples: "manage inventory across multiple locations", "automate payroll small business NZ", "reduce customer churn SaaS"

**High volume, lower conversion rate**. Users at this stage need to be educated that software is the solution before they&apos;ll convert. Works best with educational landing pages and strong remarketing.

### Category Keywords

Users searching for the category of software they need.

Examples: "project management software NZ", "accounting software small business NZ", "HR software NZ"

**Strong intent, high competition**. Major international players dominate these terms. You can still compete, especially for NZ-specific variations where local relevance matters.

### Competitor Keywords

Bidding on competitor brand names. When someone searches for a competitor, your ad appears as an alternative.

**Highest intent but restricted**: You can bid on competitor names but cannot use them in ad copy without permission. Works best for direct comparison landing pages highlighting your advantages.

### Brand Keywords

Bidding on your own brand name. Protects your brand terms from competitor campaigns and captures high-intent brand searchers.

## Campaign Structure for NZ SaaS

**Campaign 1: Core Service/Category Keywords**
Your most important campaign. Target the primary search terms that describe what your software does.

**Campaign 2: Problem/Pain Awareness**
Broader keywords targeting the problem your software solves. Requires different messaging — educational rather than product-focused.

**Campaign 3: Competitor Targeting**
For established SaaS products with known competitors. Requires dedicated comparison landing pages.

**Campaign 4: Branded**
Your own brand name. Essential protection and highly efficient spend.

**Remarketing Campaigns**
Users who visited your site but didn&apos;t convert. SaaS has a longer sales cycle — most people don&apos;t convert on the first visit. Remarketing keeps you visible as they continue their evaluation.

## Ad Copy for SaaS Google Ads

SaaS ad copy should communicate:

1. **What the software does**: Be specific, not generic
2. **Key benefit**: The primary outcome users get
3. **Proof**: Social proof, customer numbers, ratings
4. **Offer**: Free trial, free demo, money-back guarantee
5. **CTA**: Clear next step

Example for an NZ project management SaaS:

*Headline 1*: "Project Management for NZ Teams"
*Headline 2*: "Free 14-Day Trial — No Credit Card"
*Headline 3*: "Trusted by 500+ NZ Businesses"
*Description*: "Manage projects, deadlines, and team communication in one place. Made for NZ businesses. Start your free trial today."

For NZ SaaS, emphasise local: NZ-based support, NZ compliance (if relevant), understanding of the NZ market. This differentiates you from large international competitors.

## Landing Pages for SaaS PPC

Your landing page is where the campaign succeeds or fails. SaaS landing pages for Google Ads should:

**Match the keyword intent**: If someone searched "project management software NZ", the landing page should be specifically about project management, not your entire product suite.

**Lead with the free trial or demo CTA**: Make it immediately obvious how to get started. Free trial CTAs work well for self-serve products; demo request forms work better for complex or higher-priced B2B software.

**Reduce friction in the sign-up form**: For a free trial, ask for email only (or email + name). Every additional field reduces conversion rate. Qualify leads after they sign up, not before.

**Include social proof**: Customer logos (especially NZ brands), testimonials, G2/Capterra ratings, number of customers.

**Demonstrate the product**: Screenshots, video walkthroughs, or an interactive demo preview. Let people see what they&apos;re signing up for.

**Address objections**: "No credit card required." "Cancel anytime." "Takes 5 minutes to set up." Anticipate the reasons someone might hesitate.

---

## Bidding Strategy for SaaS

**Start conservative**: New SaaS campaigns benefit from Manual CPC or Target CPA with a conservative target. Build conversion history before moving to aggressive automated bidding.

**Track the right conversions**: Make sure Google is optimising for meaningful conversions (trial signups, demo requests) rather than micro-conversions (page visits, scroll depth).

**Don&apos;t optimise for volume alone**: A campaign generating 100 trial signups that none convert is worse than one generating 30 that half become paying customers. Feed CRM data back into Google Ads to optimise for revenue, not volume.

## Typical Benchmarks for NZ SaaS Google Ads

These vary significantly by category, price point, and offer:
- CPC: $5-30 (varies by keyword competitiveness)
- Conversion rate (trial/demo): 3-10%
- Cost per trial signup: $50-300
- Cost per demo request: $200-800

For SaaS businesses with annual contract values of $5,000+, these CPAs are readily justifiable.
