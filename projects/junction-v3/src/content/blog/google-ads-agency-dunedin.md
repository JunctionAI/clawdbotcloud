# Google Ads Agency Dunedin: Lower CPCs, Real Results for Otago Businesses

For Dunedin businesses considering Google Ads, there's a compelling fact that often gets overlooked in the broader New Zealand paid search conversation: cost-per-click in Dunedin is significantly lower than Auckland or Wellington for most service categories.

That difference matters. It means your Google Ads budget generates more clicks, more leads, and a lower cost per acquisition in Dunedin than it would for the equivalent campaign targeting Auckland. Combined with genuine local search demand across trades, healthcare, tourism, and professional services, Dunedin represents one of the most cost-efficient Google Ads markets in New Zealand.

---

## Why Dunedin Google Ads CPCs Are Lower

Cost-per-click in Google Ads is determined by auction competition — the more advertisers bidding on a keyword, the higher the CPC. In Auckland, most service categories have been competitive for years. Established local advertisers, national businesses targeting Auckland, and franchise operations all drive up CPCs in a crowded auction.

In Dunedin, the auction is smaller. Fewer local advertisers are running active, well-managed campaigns. National businesses often focus their paid search budgets on Auckland and Wellington, deprioritising Dunedin despite its 130,000 population. The result: lower average CPCs that make positive ROI easier to achieve.

For a Dunedin trades business spending $2,000/month in ad spend, that lower CPC translates directly into more leads at a lower cost per enquiry than the same spend in Auckland.

## Building a High-Performance Dunedin Google Ads Campaign

### Start With Keyword Strategy

Dunedin keyword strategy requires understanding local search behaviour — how Dunedin people phrase their searches, what modifiers they use, and what service areas they specify. "Electrician Dunedin South" differs from "electrician Dunedin CBD." Mosgiel searches differ from Otago Peninsula searches. This geographic nuance matters for both keyword targeting and ad messaging.

Negative keyword lists are equally important. Excluding irrelevant terms — different cities, product categories you don't offer, informational queries from users with no buying intent — keeps spend focused on the searches most likely to convert.

### Campaign Architecture That Matches Dunedin Intent

The most common Google Ads mistake we see in Dunedin businesses is poor campaign structure: broad keywords mixed with specific ones, ad groups with too many keywords and generic ad copy, no separation between different service lines.

Effective structure means tightly themed ad groups where each group targets a specific service or location, ad copy that directly reflects what the searcher is looking for, and landing pages that match the intent of the ad. A search for "plumber South Dunedin" should lead to an ad mentioning South Dunedin and a landing page that confirms you service that area.

### Geographic Targeting for Dunedin's Layout

Dunedin's geography spans from the CBD and central suburbs through South Dunedin, North Dunedin, the Otago Peninsula, and out to Mosgiel — Dunedin's fastest-growing suburb. Geographic targeting in Google Ads lets you focus spend on the specific areas your business services, and bid adjustments let you increase spend in higher-value areas.

For businesses serving the full Dunedin city, broad geographic targeting is appropriate. For trades businesses with defined service areas, tighter targeting prevents wasted spend on enquiries outside your service radius.

### Conversion Tracking: The Non-Negotiable

Running Google Ads without conversion tracking is guesswork. You need to know which keywords, ads, and campaigns are generating real business outcomes — phone calls, form submissions, bookings, purchases.

For Dunedin service businesses, call tracking is often the most important conversion metric — most enquiries come via phone. Google's call tracking integration, combined with form submission tracking, gives you clear data on which searches drive actual leads, which lets you allocate budget to what works.

## Google Ads vs SEO for Dunedin Businesses

The most common question we hear from Dunedin businesses: should I invest in Google Ads or SEO?

The honest answer: they serve different purposes and work best together.

Google Ads is fast — campaigns can generate leads within days. It's also controllable: turn it on, turn it off, adjust budget. The downside is that it stops the moment you stop paying. There's no compounding effect.

SEO is slow — results take 3–6 months to materialise — but it compounds. Organic rankings built through SEO continue to generate traffic and leads without ongoing per-click cost. The investment builds an asset.

For most Dunedin businesses, the optimal strategy is both: Google Ads for immediate lead generation while SEO compounds in the background, with the goal of transitioning more acquisition to the lower long-term cost of organic search.
