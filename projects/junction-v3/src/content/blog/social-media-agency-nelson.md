## Organic Social Media vs. Paid Social: Two Different Jobs

Many businesses confuse organic social media with paid social advertising. They're related but distinct.

### Organic Social Media

Organic social is the content you post to your profiles without paying for distribution. It reaches your existing followers and — through engagement, shares, and the algorithm — some percentage of non-followers.

The job of organic social media:

- Build brand identity and visual consistency
- Deepen relationships with existing customers
- Demonstrate expertise, personality, and values
- Generate word-of-mouth through shares and saves
- Create the content library that paid social amplifies

Organic reach on most platforms has declined significantly over the last decade. It still matters — but its primary value today is depth of relationship, not breadth of reach.

### Paid Social (Meta Ads)

Paid social is Facebook and Instagram advertising. It reaches new audiences who don't follow you, with targeting precision that organic reach can't match.

The job of paid social:

- Reach new potential customers at scale
- Drive specific actions (website visits, lead form fills, bookings)
- Retarget website visitors and warm audiences
- Test messaging before committing to organic strategy

Effective social media strategy uses both: organic builds the foundation and credibility, paid accelerates reach and conversion.
