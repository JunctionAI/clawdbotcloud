## Why Napier Google Ads CPCs Are Competitive

Cost-per-click in Google Ads is set by auction. More advertisers competing for the same keywords drives CPCs up. In Auckland, most service categories are highly competitive — established local advertisers, national companies targeting the region, and franchise networks all participate in the same auction, pushing costs higher.

In Napier, the auction is smaller and less competitive. Fewer businesses are running active, well-managed Google Ads campaigns. National advertisers frequently focus their paid search budgets on Auckland and Wellington, treating secondary cities as secondary priorities. The result is lower average CPCs that make positive return on investment genuinely achievable for smaller budgets.

A Napier trades business spending $1,500/month in Google Ads can generate a meaningful volume of qualified leads because each click costs substantially less than it would in a major centre.

## Building a Napier Google Ads Campaign That Works

### Keyword Strategy for Hawke's Bay

Effective Napier Google Ads starts with understanding how Hawke's Bay people actually search. The geographic nuances matter: someone searching "builder Napier" may want results near the CBD, while "builder Havelock North" or "builder Hastings" signals different location expectations. Trade service searches often include specific suburb or area qualifiers.

Wine and tourism searches have seasonal patterns tied to harvest season, summer holidays, and major regional events like the Art Deco Festival. Aligning campaign budgets to these patterns — spending more when search volume peaks — improves efficiency.

Negative keywords are as important as positive ones. Excluding searches for locations you don't serve, services you don't offer, and informational queries from people who have no buying intent keeps your spend focused on the searches most likely to generate real business.

### Campaign Structure for Local Napier Businesses

The most common Google Ads mistakes in Napier businesses involve poor structure: too many different services crammed into a single campaign, broad keywords competing with specific ones, ad copy that doesn't match what the searcher is looking for. 

Best practice means separating services into distinct campaigns (or tightly themed ad groups), writing ad copy that directly addresses what the search term suggests the person needs, and sending traffic to landing pages that confirm you offer exactly what was searched. A search for "electrician Greenmeadows" should lead to an ad mentioning that suburb and a page confirming you service that area.

### Geographic Targeting Across Hawke's Bay

Napier businesses typically serve the wider Hawke's Bay area — not just the Napier CBD. Google Ads geographic targeting lets you define your service area precisely and focus spend on the locations that matter. For businesses serving all of Hawke's Bay (Napier, Hastings, Havelock North, Wairoa), a regional targeting approach works. For businesses with a tighter service radius, more precise targeting prevents wasted spend on enquiries you can't service.

Location bid adjustments let you increase spend in higher-value areas — for example, increasing bids for Havelock North if you know conversion rates are higher there.

### Conversion Tracking: Non-Negotiable

Without conversion tracking, Google Ads is expensive guesswork. You need to know which keywords and ads generate real business outcomes — phone calls, form submissions, online bookings. For most Napier service businesses, phone call tracking is the most critical metric: most leads come in via phone.

Proper conversion tracking — set up before campaigns launch — gives you the data to allocate budget to what works, cut what doesn't, and continuously improve campaign performance over time.

## Google Ads vs SEO for Napier Businesses

The most common question we hear: should I invest in Google Ads or SEO? 

**Google Ads** delivers fast results — campaigns can generate leads within days of launching. It's controllable and scalable. The downside: it stops when you stop paying, and there's no compounding effect.

**SEO** is slower but compounds. Organic rankings built through SEO generate traffic and leads without per-click cost. The investment creates a durable asset.

For most Napier businesses, the optimal approach is both: Google Ads for immediate lead generation while SEO builds in the background. As organic rankings improve, you can reduce reliance on paid traffic — but in the meantime, you're not leaving leads on the table waiting for SEO to kick in.
