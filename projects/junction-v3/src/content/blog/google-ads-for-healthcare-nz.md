# Google Ads for Healthcare NZ: Driving Patient Appointments With Compliant PPC Campaigns

When a New Zealander needs a new GP, physio, dentist, or specialist, Google is where they start. They search, compare a few options, click the most credible result, and book. If your healthcare practice isn&apos;t appearing prominently in those search results, you&apos;re invisible at the exact moment a new patient is looking for you.

Google Ads allows healthcare providers to appear at the top of search results for the exact terms patients are searching. Done correctly, it&apos;s one of the fastest ways to fill your appointment book with new patients.

---

## The Healthcare Google Ads Opportunity in NZ

New Zealand has a significant supply-demand imbalance in healthcare. Many practices have waitlists; others struggle to attract new patients in competitive urban markets. Google Ads serves both situations:

**For busy practices with waitlists**: Google Ads can be targeted to attract the specific patient types you want more of — perhaps higher-value private patients, specific treatment types, or patients from particular demographics.

**For growing practices**: Google Ads delivers immediate visibility that SEO can take 6-12 months to build. A new physio clinic or dental practice can start attracting patients within weeks of going live with campaigns.

**For specialists**: Referral-dependent specialists can use Google Ads to supplement referrals and educate GPs and patients about their services.

## Google&apos;s Healthcare Advertising Policies

Healthcare is one of Google&apos;s most regulated advertising categories. Understanding the restrictions is essential before investing budget.

**General healthcare advertising**: Google allows advertising for most legitimate healthcare services, including general practice, dental, physiotherapy, psychology, and most allied health services.

**Prescription medications**: Advertising prescription medications directly to consumers is not permitted in most jurisdictions, including NZ.

**Clinical claims**: Be cautious about any advertising claims regarding treatment outcomes, cure rates, or guarantees. These can trigger policy violations and — more importantly — may not be accurate.

**Personalised advertising restrictions**: Google restricts targeting based on health conditions. You can target by search intent (what someone is searching for), but not by health status inferred from their browsing history.

**Certification requirements**: Some healthcare categories require Google certification. Research the requirements for your specific service category.

The safest approach is to focus ads on the service you provide (physiotherapy in Wellington) rather than specific medical claims.

## Campaign Structure for Healthcare Practices

### Service-Specific Campaigns

Rather than one broad "healthcare" campaign, structure campaigns around specific services. A multi-disciplinary clinic might run separate campaigns for:
- General practice / GP services
- Physiotherapy
- Dental
- Psychology/counselling
- Specialist services

This allows precise keyword targeting and ad copy that matches exactly what the patient is searching for.

### Geographic Targeting

Healthcare is inherently local. People don&apos;t travel far for a GP or dentist. Structure your geographic targeting to match your genuine catchment area.

For Auckland, Wellington, or Christchurch practices, you might target specific suburbs or a radius around your clinic. For rural or regional practitioners, wider geographic targeting might be appropriate.

### High-Intent Keyword Examples

Search terms that indicate strong intent for healthcare appointments:
- "[specialty] [suburb/city] appointment"
- "new [specialty] taking patients [city]"
- "best [specialty] near me"
- "[specialty] NZ cost"
- "[condition] treatment [city]"

Also target your specific practice name and the names of your practitioners — people may have heard of you and be searching directly.

## Ad Copy That Converts Healthcare Searchers

Healthcare ad copy needs to combine information with trust. Patients are making decisions that affect their health — they need to feel confident in your practice.

Effective elements for healthcare ad copy:
- **Qualifications**: "Board-Registered Physiotherapists"
- **Availability**: "Appointments Available This Week"
- **Location specificity**: "Central Wellington Clinic — Easy Parking"
- **Patient-focused language**: "Friendly, Experienced Team"
- **Social proof**: "Trusted by 2,000+ Auckland Patients"
- **Clear CTA**: "Book Online" or "Call to Enquire"

Avoid: vague superlatives, guarantees of treatment outcomes, or anything that could mislead a patient about your services.

### Ad Extensions

Healthcare advertisers should use all available extensions:
- **Call extensions**: Make it easy for patients to call directly from the ad
- **Location extensions**: Show your clinic address for local trust signals
- **Sitelink extensions**: Links to specific services, online booking, and meet the team
- **Callout extensions**: "ACC Registered", "Online Bookings Available", "Same-Day Appointments"

## Landing Pages for Healthcare Google Ads

The landing page experience determines whether someone clicks through and becomes a patient. Healthcare landing pages must:

**Build immediate credibility**: Professional photos of your team and clinic, credentials and registrations prominently displayed, patient testimonials (with appropriate consent).

**Make booking frictionless**: A prominent online booking button or phone number above the fold. Don&apos;t make patients search for how to book.

**Provide enough information to reassure**: Services offered, what to expect at a first appointment, pricing or bulk billing information.

**Mobile-optimised**: The majority of NZ healthcare searches happen on mobile. A poor mobile experience loses patients.

**Fast loading**: Every second of load time increases bounce rate. Healthcare landing pages should load in under 3 seconds.

## Tracking Patient Conversions

Effective healthcare Google Ads requires tracking the actions that matter:

**Phone call tracking**: Most healthcare bookings start with a phone call. Google&apos;s call tracking attributes calls back to specific campaigns.

**Online booking tracking**: If you use an online booking system, set up conversion tracking for completed bookings.

**Form submissions**: Track enquiry form submissions as conversions.

Without conversion tracking, you&apos;re flying blind. You have no way to know which keywords or campaigns are generating patients vs. just generating clicks.
