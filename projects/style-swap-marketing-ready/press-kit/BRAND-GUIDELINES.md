## 1. Brand Overview

### Brand Essence
Style Swap is the smart, sustainable, and effortless way to shop for clothes online. We're powered by AI but centered on humans—empowering everyone to look and feel their best without the waste, cost, or frustration of returns.

### Brand Personality
- **Innovative** - Cutting-edge AI technology made accessible
- **Confident** - Empowers users to make better purchase decisions
- **Approachable** - Friendly, not intimidating or overly technical
- **Sustainable** - Environmentally conscious without being preachy
- **Playful** - Fun and engaging, not boring or corporate

### Brand Promise
*See yourself in any outfit before you buy—guaranteed accurate, instant, and waste-free.*

<a name="logo-usage"></a>
## 2. Logo Usage

### Primary Logo

**Full Color Logo**
- Use on white or light backgrounds
- Preferred version for most applications
- File: `style-swap-logo-full-color.svg`

**Specifications:**
- Minimum width: 120px (digital), 1 inch (print)
- Clear space: Minimum 20px on all sides
- Do not stretch, skew, or alter proportions

### Logo Variations

**Wordmark Only**
- Use when icon is already present or space is limited
- File: `style-swap-wordmark.svg`

**Icon Only**
- Use for app icon, social avatars, favicons
- File: `style-swap-icon.svg`
- Minimum size: 32x32px

**Reversed Logo (White)**
- Use on dark or purple/pink gradient backgrounds
- File: `style-swap-logo-reversed.svg`

**Monochrome Logo**
- Use when color reproduction is limited
- File: `style-swap-logo-mono.svg`

### Logo Don'ts
❌ Do not change logo colors  
❌ Do not add effects (shadows, outlines, gradients)  
❌ Do not rotate or distort  
❌ Do not place on busy backgrounds without proper contrast  
❌ Do not recreate or modify the logo

<a name="typography"></a>
## 4. Typography

### Primary Font: Montserrat

**Montserrat Black** (900)
- Usage: Large headlines, hero text, emphasis
- Sizes: 48pt+

**Montserrat Bold** (700)
- Usage: Headlines, section titles, CTAs
- Sizes: 24-48pt

**Montserrat SemiBold** (600)
- Usage: Subheadings, labels, navigation
- Sizes: 16-24pt

**Montserrat Regular** (400)
- Usage: Body text, paragraphs
- Sizes: 14-18pt

**Montserrat Light** (300)
- Usage: Captions, small text, disclaimers
- Sizes: 12-14pt

### Fallback Fonts
- **Web:** `'Montserrat', 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif`
- **System:** -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI'

### Typography Guidelines

**Headlines**
- Font: Montserrat Bold or Black
- Color: Dark Gray (#1F2937) or Purple (#6B46C1)
- Line Height: 1.2
- Letter Spacing: -0.02em (tighter for large text)

**Body Text**
- Font: Montserrat Regular
- Color: Medium Gray (#6B7280)
- Line Height: 1.6
- Font Size: 16px (desktop), 14px (mobile)

**Links**
- Color: Purple (#6B46C1)
- Hover: Pink (#EC4899)
- Underline: Optional (recommended for accessibility)

**Buttons**
- Font: Montserrat SemiBold or Bold
- All Caps: Optional (use sparingly)
- Letter Spacing: 0.05em for all caps

<a name="photography"></a>
## 5. Photography & Imagery

### Photography Style

**General Guidelines:**
- Bright, natural lighting (avoid harsh shadows)
- Vibrant but realistic colors (not oversaturated)
- Diverse representation (body types, ethnicities, ages, genders)
- Authentic moments (not overly staged or stock-looking)
- Clean, minimal backgrounds (avoid clutter)

**Subject Matter:**
- Real people using the app
- Before/after transformations
- Fashion-forward, relatable outfits
- Lifestyle shots (shopping, getting ready, social situations)

**Forbidden:**
- Generic stock photography
- Overly airbrushed or edited images
- Outdated or low-resolution images
- Images that don't represent brand diversity

---

### AI Try-On Examples

**Quality Standards:**
- High resolution (1080px minimum width)
- Photorealistic AI results only
- Clear before/after comparison when applicable
- Diverse body types showcased
- Various fashion styles represented

---

### Iconography

**Style:**
- Outlined icons (not filled)
- 2px stroke weight
- Rounded corners (2px radius)
- Simple, minimalist design
- Consistent sizing (24x24px, 32x32px, 48x48px)

**Color:**
- Primary: Purple (#6B46C1)
- On dark backgrounds: White (#FFFFFF)
- Inactive: Light Gray (#9CA3AF)

<a name="voice-tone"></a>
## 6. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice

**Characteristics:**
- **Clear & Direct:** No jargon, straightforward messaging
- **Confident:** We know our tech works, and we're not shy about it
- **Friendly:** Approachable, warm, conversational (not corporate)
- **Empowering:** Focus on what users gain, not just features
- **Playful:** Use humor and personality (without being unprofessional)

### Tone Variations by Context

**Marketing/Social Media**
- More playful and energetic
- Use emojis (moderately)
- Internet-savvy language ("fr fr," "it's giving," etc.)
- Casual and conversational

**Product UI/UX**
- Clear and concise
- Helpful and supportive
- Minimal jargon
- Action-oriented

**Customer Support**
- Empathetic and patient
- Solution-focused
- Professional but warm
- Reassuring

**Press/Corporate**
- Professional and credible
- Data-driven
- Industry-aware
- Confident but not boastful

---

### Writing Style Guide

**Do:**
- Use contractions (we're, you'll, it's)
- Write in second person ("you" and "your")
- Keep sentences short and scannable
- Use active voice
- Be specific with numbers and benefits
- End with clear calls-to-action

**Don't:**
- Use jargon or technical terms without explanation
- Write long, complex sentences
- Use passive voice
- Be vague or ambiguous
- Make unrealistic promises
- Use gendered language unnecessarily

<a name="social-media"></a>
## 8. Social Media Guidelines

### General Rules

**Post Frequency:**
- Instagram: 4-5 times/week (Feed), Daily (Stories)
- TikTok: 3-5 times/week
- Twitter: 1-2 times/day
- LinkedIn: 2-3 times/week

**Hashtag Strategy:**
- Always include: #StyleSwap
- 5-10 relevant hashtags per post
- Mix high-volume (#fashion) + niche (#virtualtryon)
- Create branded hashtags for campaigns

**Voice:**
- Conversational and relatable
- Use emojis (but don't overdo it)
- Respond to comments within 24 hours
- Engage authentically (no generic replies)

---

### Platform-Specific Guidelines

**Instagram**
- Aesthetic: Bright, colorful, visually cohesive grid
- Captions: 150-300 words, engaging hook, clear CTA
- Stories: Behind-the-scenes, polls, Q&As, user features
- Reels: 15-30 seconds, trending sounds, high energy

**TikTok**
- Format: Vertical video, hook in first 3 seconds
- Content: Relatable, educational, entertaining
- Sounds: Use trending audio, create challenges
- Duets/Stitches: Engage with user content

**Twitter**
- Tone: Witty, timely, conversational
- Length: Short and punchy (under 280 characters)
- Engagement: Reply, retweet, participate in trends
- Threads: Use for deeper storytelling

**LinkedIn**
- Tone: Professional, industry-focused
- Content: Company updates, thought leadership, hiring
- Format: Text posts, articles, company culture

<a name="dos-donts"></a>
## 9. Do's and Don'ts

### Visual Do's and Don'ts

✅ **Do:**
- Use high-quality, diverse imagery
- Maintain consistent brand colors
- Keep designs clean and uncluttered
- Ensure text is readable (contrast, size)
- Use gradient on large background areas
- Showcase real user transformations

❌ **Don't:**
- Use outdated or low-res images
- Alter logo colors or proportions
- Use conflicting design styles
- Overcrowd designs with too much text
- Use generic stock photography
- Misrepresent AI accuracy (no fake results)

---

### Messaging Do's and Don'ts

✅ **Do:**
- Focus on user benefits
- Use clear, simple language
- Include specific data points
- Encourage action with strong CTAs
- Acknowledge environmental impact
- Celebrate user success stories

❌ **Don't:**
- Make unrealistic promises
- Use fear-based messaging
- Over-explain the technology
- Ignore competitor strengths
- Shame users about returns
- Use gendered or exclusive language

---

### Partnership Do's and Don'ts

✅ **Do:**
- Follow partner brand guidelines when co-creating
- Get approval before using partner logos
- Tag and credit partners appropriately
- Align messaging for co-branded campaigns
- Disclose sponsored content (FTC compliance)

❌ **Don't:**
- Misrepresent partnership relationships
- Use partner assets without permission
- Create conflicting brand messages
- Make exclusive claims without agreements
