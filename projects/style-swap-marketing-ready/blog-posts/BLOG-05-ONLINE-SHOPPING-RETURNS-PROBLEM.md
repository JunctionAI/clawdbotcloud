## Why Do We Return So Much?

### Reason #1: It Doesn't Fit (70% of returns)

**The Problem:**
- Sizing inconsistency across brands
- "Medium" at Brand A ≠ "Medium" at Brand B
- Online size charts are confusing or inaccurate
- We guess wrong

**The Behavior:**
- Order 3 sizes, keep 1, return 2
- "Better safe than sorry" mentality
- Waste of time, money, and resources

---

### Reason #2: It Looks Different Than Expected (15% of returns)

**The Problem:**
- Professional photos use perfect lighting and editing
- Models have different body types than us
- Colors look different on screens vs. real life
- "Photoshop vs. reality" disappointment

**The Behavior:**
- Order based on how it looks on model
- Receive item, disappointed
- Return and try again

---

### Reason #3: Impulse Buying / "Just in Case" Ordering (10% of returns)

**The Problem:**
- Easy checkout (one-click ordering)
- Free returns policies encourage over-ordering
- "I might need this someday" mentality
- FOMO (fear of missing out on sales)

**The Behavior:**
- Add everything to cart
- Order without thinking
- Realize later it's unnecessary
- Return in bulk

---

### Reason #4: Changed Mind / Found Something Better (5% of returns)

**The Problem:**
- Too much choice (analysis paralysis)
- Continue shopping AFTER ordering
- Buyer's remorse

## How Retailers Actually Hate Returns (But Won't Tell You)

### The Retailer's Hidden Costs

**Processing One Return:**
- Inspect item (labor cost)
- Repackage and restock (labor + materials)
- Mark down if damaged (lost revenue)
- Dispose if unsellable (waste cost)

**Total:** $10-30 per return

For fast fashion brands, this often exceeds the item's value—so they landfill it.

---

### "Free Returns" Aren't Free

**Who Pays?**
- Retailers build return costs into prices (you pay upfront)
- "Free shipping both ways" = everyone's prices increase 10-15%
- Non-returners subsidize serial returners

**The Paradox:**
- Easy returns encourage over-ordering
- Over-ordering inflates returns
- Returns increase prices
- Cycle repeats

### Strategy #2: Read Reviews Obsessively

**What to Look For:**
- Reviews from people with YOUR body type
- Fit feedback ("runs small," "true to size," "stretchy")
- Photo reviews (real people, real lighting)
- Filter by "verified purchase"

**Pro Tip:** Sort by "most helpful" for honest reviews.

---

### Strategy #3: Know Your Measurements (And Use Them)

**One-Time Setup:**
- Measure yourself (bust, waist, hips, inseam)
- Store measurements in notes app
- Reference for every purchase

**Compare to Size Charts:**
- Always check brand's specific size chart
- Don't assume "your size" fits everywhere
- When in doubt, size up

---

### Strategy #4: Set a "24-Hour Rule" for Impulse Buys

**How It Works:**
- Add item to cart (or wishlist)
- Wait 24 hours before purchasing
- Re-evaluate: "Do I still want this?"

**Result:**
- 60% of impulse items get removed
- You only buy what you truly want
- Reduces regret returns

---

### Strategy #5: Follow the "One In, One Out" Rule

**The Rule:**
- For every new item, remove one from closet
- Forces intentional purchasing
- Prevents wardrobe clutter
- Reduces "just in case" buying

---

### Strategy #6: Shop Brands You Trust

**Build Your "Good Fit" List:**
- Track brands that consistently fit you well
- Note which brands run small/large
- Prioritize those brands for future purchases

**Result:**
- Higher confidence per purchase
- Fewer size-related returns
- Faster shopping decisions

## When Returns Are Actually Necessary

**Legitimate Reasons to Return:**
- Item defective or damaged
- Wrong item shipped
- Significantly different from description (false advertising)

**How to Minimize Even These:**
- Buy from reputable retailers
- Read return policies before purchasing
- Inspect items immediately upon arrival
- Report issues within return window
