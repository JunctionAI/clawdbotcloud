### Cut Style Rules
- Shots 1–3: **Slower cuts** (build, don't rush — 4-5 sec each)
- Shots 4–5: **Hard cuts, no transition** (action hits = no dissolves)

## STEP 3 — Sound Design

**Rule: No music for the first 10 seconds. Nature sounds only. Then build.**

### Layers to Add (CapCut free audio / Epidemic Sound / Pixabay)

**0:00–0:11 (Establish + Gorilla eyes + Soldiers advance)**
- Wind. Low hum. Desert ambience.
- Boots on gravel (approaching, slow)
- Distant military comms crackle (very low)
- Gorilla breathing (slow, massive)

**0:11–0:24 (First contact + Chaos)**
- IMPACT: Deep sub-bass punch on Shot 4 (first hit)
- Percussion: Military drum build (Taiko style)
- Crowd chaos sounds: shouting, equipment clanging
- Bullet sounds: realistic, not Hollywood

**0:25–0:34 (Tide turns + Roar)**
- Music swells: Orchestral (strings + brass) — enters here
- GORILLA ROAR: Download real silverback gorilla roar
  - Source: freesound.org search "gorilla roar" or "silverback"
  - Layer 2-3 recordings for depth
- Slow-mo audio effect on Shot 7 (pitch shift down 20%)

**0:34–0:52 (Last stand + Devastation + Aftermath)**
- Music: Full orchestral peak — think Hans Zimmer "Inception" style
- Impact sounds: Layered hits for each body that falls
- Shot 9b (ground silence): Music drops OUT — just silence + dust settling

**0:52–1:04 (Final eyes + Title card)**
- Single sustained cello or low drone
- Shot 11: Complete silence with very faint wind
- Shot 12 title: Low orchestral swell, hold as text appears, fade out

### Free Music Recommendations
- **Pixabay:** Search "epic orchestral" or "cinematic battle"
- **YouTube Audio Library:** "Trailer" genre, filter by intensity
- **Freesound.org:** For individual SFX (gorilla roar, impacts, wind)
- **Recommended track style:** Hans Zimmer-adjacent, no lyrics, no pop

## STEP 5 — Color Grade (Optional but recommended)

Quick grade to unify the AI-generated clips:

**In CapCut (Filters → Cinematic):**
- Apply "FILM" or "DARK" preset to all clips
- Reduce saturation by 10-15%
- Boost contrast slightly (+10)
- Add slight vignette (edges darker)
- Warm up highlights slightly (golden tones)

**In DaVinci (Color page):**
- Create a node: Contrast +0.1, Saturation -0.1
- Add a LUT: "Kodak 2383" or similar film emulation
- Push shadows toward teal, highlights toward warm amber

**Goal:** Make all 15 clips feel like they're from the same world, same film stock.
