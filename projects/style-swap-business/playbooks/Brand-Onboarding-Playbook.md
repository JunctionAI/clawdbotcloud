## Week 1: Discovery & Agreement

### Day 1-2: Initial Discovery Call

**Objectives:**
- Understand their business challenges
- Identify inventory opportunities
- Gauge sustainability commitment
- Determine best-fit partnership tier

**Discovery Questions:**
```
Business Understanding:
- What's your current approach to excess inventory?
- What percentage of seasonal inventory typically goes unsold?
- How do you currently handle returns?
- What are your sustainability goals for [current year]?

Inventory Assessment:
- How many SKUs do you produce seasonally?
- What's your average retail price point?
- What categories have the most excess (tops, bottoms, accessories)?
- Do you have warehouse space constraints?

Partnership Fit:
- Have you worked with resale platforms before?
- What concerns do you have about brand dilution?
- What would success look like for this partnership?
- What timeline are you considering?
```

**Call Follow-Up (Within 24 hours):**
- [ ] Send meeting summary email
- [ ] Attach customized ROI projection
- [ ] Include 2-3 relevant case studies
- [ ] Provide tier recommendation with rationale
- [ ] Schedule follow-up call

### Day 3-5: Proposal & Negotiation

**Send Formal Proposal Including:**
1. Executive summary of partnership opportunity
2. Recommended tier with justification
3. Projected revenue model (conservative estimate)
4. Timeline to launch
5. Draft partnership agreement
6. Next steps

**Negotiation Points:**
- Commission structure (within tier guidelines)
- Inventory volume commitments
- Marketing collaboration opportunities
- Custom terms for large brands (Gold tier)

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Unrealistic revenue expectations
- Quality control resistance
- Unwillingness to share basic data
- Price manipulation concerns

### Day 6-7: Agreement Execution

**Finalize Agreement:**
- [ ] Incorporate negotiated terms
- [ ] Legal review (both sides)
- [ ] Execute signature (DocuSign or similar)
- [ ] File in partnership database
- [ ] Trigger onboarding workflow

**Kickoff Email Template:**

### Performance Optimization

**Metrics to Track:**
```
Sales Metrics:
- Total revenue
- Items sold / sell-through rate
- Average order value
- Conversion rate (views to sales)

Inventory Metrics:
- Days to first sale
- Inventory turnover rate
- Aged inventory (>60 days)
- Return rate

Customer Metrics:
- New vs. repeat buyers
- Customer demographics
- Product reviews/ratings
- Customer service inquiries
```

**Optimization Actions:**

**If slow-moving inventory (>45 days):**
1. Analyze pricing vs. market
2. Improve listing photos/description
3. Feature in email campaign
4. Consider strategic discount (with brand approval)
5. Refresh listing with new photography

**If high-performing items:**
1. Request similar inventory
2. Feature in marketing
3. Consider raising prices for next batch
4. Create case study
5. Cross-promote to expand audience

**If low conversion (high views, low sales):**
1. Review pricing strategy
2. Enhance product descriptions
3. Add lifestyle photography
4. Improve size guides
5. Address customer concerns in Q&A

## Troubleshooting & FAQs

### Common Challenges & Solutions

#### Challenge: Brand Hesitant About Pricing

**Solution:**
- Start with brand's recommended pricing
- Monitor performance for 2 weeks
- Present data-driven pricing recommendations
- Test A/B pricing on similar items
- Show competitive analysis
- Emphasize revenue maximization over price protection

#### Challenge: Inventory Not Moving

**Solution:**
- Conduct listing audit (photos, descriptions, pricing)
- Feature in targeted email campaign
- Offer limited-time promotion
- Reposition items (seasonal, occasion-based)
- Consider bundle opportunities
- Last resort: Discuss discount or return to brand

#### Challenge: Quality Complaints

**Solution:**
- Immediate QC review process
- Contact brand partner within 24 hours
- Tighten intake inspection standards
- Provide feedback loop
- Update listing descriptions to be more accurate
- Offer return/refund to customer

#### Challenge: Brand Wants to Terminate Early

**Solution:**
- Schedule urgent call to understand concerns
- Review contract terms and obligations
- Present performance data objectively
- Offer solutions to address specific issues
- Consider tier adjustment
- If genuine misalignment, facilitate graceful exit

### FAQ for Brand Partners

**Q: How quickly will my products sell?**
A: Typical sell-through rates: 30-40% in first 30 days, 60-70% in 90 days. Variables include pricing, season, brand recognition, and product category.

**Q: Can I control pricing?**
A: You provide recommended pricing; we collaborate on final pricing based on market data. Items over $500 require mutual approval for price changes.

**Q: What happens to unsold items?**
A: After 90 days, options are: (1) continue listing, (2) strategic discount, (3) return to you, (4) donate. We decide together.

**Q: How do you handle returns?**
A: Customer returns within 14 days for any reason. Items are re-inspected and re-listed if in good condition. No commission charged on returned items.

**Q: Can I promote this partnership publicly?**
A: Yes! We encourage it. We provide co-marketing assets and approve partnership mentions. Great for sustainability reports.

**Q: What if I want to upgrade/downgrade my tier?**
A: You can change tiers at any renewal period (after initial term) with 30 days notice. We'll adjust commission and benefits accordingly.

**Q: How do I get paid?**
A: Monthly, within 15 days of month-end. Minimum payout is $100. You'll receive a detailed sales report with each payment.
