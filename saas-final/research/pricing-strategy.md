# Pricing Strategy - Research & Final Model

**Goal:** Maximize revenue while remaining accessible  
**Model:** Value-based pricing (based on time saved)  
**Status:** Ready to Launch

---

## Pricing Philosophy

### Value-Based (Not Cost-Based)

**Bad pricing (cost-plus):**
```
Infrastructure costs: $2/customer/month
Desired margin: 80%
Price: $10/month ❌
```

**Good pricing (value-based):**
```
Time saved: 20 hours/month
Customer hourly rate: $50/hour
Value created: $1,000/month
Price we capture: $199-999/month (10-90% of value) ✅
```

**Rule:** Price based on value delivered, not costs incurred.

## Pricing Psychology

### Anchoring (High to Low)

**Our Pricing Page Order:**
1. Enterprise ($999) - Anchor
2. Professional ($499) - Featured (most popular)
3. Starter ($199) - Entry

**Why this works:**
- Enterprise anchors high (makes $499 feel reasonable)
- Professional in middle (highlighted as "best value")
- Starter feels affordable (comparison to $999)

### Decoy Pricing

**If we wanted to push Professional:**
- Starter: $199 (limited)
- Starter Plus: $299 (slightly better) ← Decoy
- Professional: $499 (much better value) ← Target

**How it works:**
- Starter Plus is intentionally bad value
- Makes Professional look like obvious choice
- Increases conversion to $499 tier (higher revenue)

**Decision:** Not using decoy for now (keep it simple)

## Discounts & Promotions

### Launch Discount (Month 1 Only)
- 50% off first month ("Launch Special")
- Applied automatically at checkout
- Creates urgency ("Limited time")

**Impact:**
- Increases signups (lower barrier)
- Reduces initial revenue (acceptable trade-off)
- Month 2 renewals at full price

---

### Annual Plans (Always Available)
- 20% discount (pay 10 months, get 12)
- Improves cash flow (upfront payment)
- Reduces churn (12-month commitment)

**Pricing:**
- Starter: $1,910/year (vs $2,388 monthly)
- Professional: $4,790/year (vs $5,988 monthly)
- Enterprise: $9,590/year (vs $11,988 monthly)

---

## Upgrade/Downgrade Flow

### Upgrade
**Trigger:** Customer clicks "Upgrade" in billing page

**Flow:**
1. Show feature comparison
2. Calculate prorated charge
3. Confirm with user
4. Charge card (Stripe)
5. Unlock features immediately

**Proration example:**
- Day 15 of Starter ($199) → Upgrade to Professional ($499)
- Remaining value: $199 * 15/30 = $99.50
- New plan charge: $499
- Amount due: $499 - $99.50 = $399.50

---

### Downgrade
**Trigger:** Customer clicks "Downgrade" in billing page

**Flow:**
1. Show feature loss warning
2. Calculate credit
3. Confirm with user
4. Downgrade at end of billing cycle (not immediate)
5. Credit applied to next invoice

**Why not immediate:**
- User paid for full month
- Fair to honor full period
- Reduces support burden (no refunds)

## Failed Payment Handling

### Dunning Strategy

**Attempt 1 (Day 0):**
- Stripe auto-retries
- Email: "Payment failed, update card"

**Attempt 2 (Day 3):**
- Stripe retries again
- Email: "Second attempt in 3 days, update card now"

**Attempt 3 (Day 7):**
- Stripe final retry
- Email: "Final attempt tomorrow, account will suspend"

**Day 10:**
- Suspend account (keep data 30 days)
- Email: "Account suspended, update card to reactivate"

**Recovery rate:** 30-40% (industry average)

## Price Increase Strategy (Future)

### When to Raise Prices
- **Milestone:** 500 customers (proven product-market fit)
- **Timing:** Give 60 days notice
- **Grandfathering:** Lock in current customers at old price (loyalty)

### How Much
- 10-20% increase (not 50%+, too shocking)
- Example: Starter $199 → $229, Professional $499 → $599

### Communication
```
Subject: Clawdbot Pricing Update (You're Grandfathered In)

Hi [NAME],

We're increasing prices for new customers starting March 1:
- Starter: $199 → $229
- Professional: $499 → $599
- Enterprise: $999 → $1,199

The good news? Your price stays the same as long as you remain a customer.

Why the increase? We've added 20+ new integrations and features. New customers will pay more, but you get them for free.

Thanks for being an early supporter!
— Tom
```

**Churn impact:** <5% (most accept grandfathering)
