## ⚖️ LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Statute of Limitations (NZ)
- **Standard limitation:** 6 years from cause of action
- **Relevance here:** LOW - This is not a debt collection; it's a credit reporting dispute
- **Credit file entries:** Generally stay for 5 years, but ERRORS must be corrected regardless

### Applicable Legislation
1. **Credit Reporting Privacy Code 2020**
   - Data accuracy requirements
   - Right to request correction
   - Complaint process to Privacy Commissioner

2. **Fair Trading Act 1986**
   - Misleading/deceptive conduct
   - Applies to credit reporting

3. **Privacy Act 2020**
   - Right to access and correct personal information
   - Complaint mechanism through Privacy Commissioner

4. **Consumer Guarantees Act 1993**
   - Services must be performed with reasonable care and skill

### Regulatory Bodies
- **Privacy Commissioner** - For credit reporting errors
- **FSCL (Financial Services Complaints Ltd)** - Financial services disputes
- **Commerce Commission** - Fair Trading Act breaches
- **Banking Ombudsman** - If bank-related aspects exist

## 🔧 STRATEGY OPTIONS

### Option A: Regulatory Complaint Path (RECOMMENDED)
**Approach:** File formal complaints with Privacy Commissioner and FSCL
**Pros:**
- Free
- Official investigation
- Can result in binding orders for correction
- Creates paper trail for potential damages claim
**Cons:**
- Can take 3-6 months
- No direct financial compensation (usually)
**Likelihood of success:** HIGH - 2 years of documented unresponsiveness is compelling

### Option B: Direct Negotiation with Escalation
**Approach:** Send final demand letter threatening regulatory + legal action
**Pros:**
- May get quick resolution
- Less administrative burden
**Cons:**
- Latitude has proven unresponsive
- May need to escalate anyway
**Likelihood of success:** MEDIUM - History suggests they ignore correspondence

### Option C: Small Claims / Disputes Tribunal
**Approach:** File claim for damages caused by credit reporting error
**Jurisdiction:** Disputes Tribunal (claims up to $30,000)
**Potential damages:**
- Emotional distress / stress
- Time spent on correspondence
- Any loan/credit denials due to error
- Any interest rate impacts
**Pros:**
- Can get financial compensation
- Formal legal ruling
**Cons:**
- Need to quantify damages
- Filing fees
- Time investment
**Likelihood of success:** MEDIUM-HIGH with documented 2-year history

### Option D: Community Law Centre Consultation
**Approach:** Free legal advice session
**Website:** https://communitylaw.org.nz/
**Purpose:** Get professional guidance before escalating
**Pros:** Free, professional advice
**Cons:** May have wait time for appointment
