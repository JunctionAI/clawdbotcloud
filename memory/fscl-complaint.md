## NATURE OF COMPLAINT

### Primary Issues

1. **Credit Reporting Error** — Latitude Finance has reported incorrect arrears/missed payment information to Centrix (New Zealand's credit bureau), damaging my credit file. The erroneous report relates to a payment that was made on time but recorded as "missed" due to a processing issue or outage on Latitude's end.

2. **Failure to Respond and Resolve** — Despite nearly **two years** of complaints, escalations, and documented correspondence (May 2024 – present), Latitude Finance has failed to correct the credit reporting error, respond meaningfully to my complaints, or engage in any reasonable dispute resolution process.

3. **Systemic Unresponsiveness** — Latitude has consistently failed to respond to Centrix's requests for clarification, my direct complaints, and escalated "urgent" notices, demonstrating a pattern of neglecting consumer complaints.

## SUMMARY OF LATITUDE'S FAILURES

### 1. Failure to Correct Credit Reporting Error

Latitude reported incorrect information to Centrix (a payment made on time recorded as "missed") and has **failed to correct this error for nearly two years** despite:
- Centrix contacting them multiple times on my behalf
- Multiple urgent follow-up requests from Centrix
- My direct complaints to Latitude (10+ times by my count)
- My escalation emails and "final notice" warning

### 2. Failure to Respond

Latitude has demonstrated a pattern of failing to respond:
- Did not respond within Centrix's stated 20 business day timeframe
- Did not respond to Centrix's September 2024 urgent request
- Did not respond to Centrix's November 2024 urgent request
- Did not respond substantively to my April 2025 urgent escalation
- Responded only with an automated reply to my June 2025 final notice
- Has never provided a substantive written response explaining their position or timeline for resolution

### 3. Buck-Passing Between Parties

When I contacted Latitude directly (10+ times), they directed me to Centrix. When Centrix contacted Latitude, Latitude failed to respond. This created an impossible situation where:
- Latitude would not resolve the issue directly
- Latitude would not respond to Centrix's requests
- I was left in the middle with no path to resolution

## WHAT I BELIEVE LATITUDE DID WRONG

1. **Reported inaccurate information to Centrix** — A payment I made on time was incorrectly recorded as missed due to a processing issue on their end.

2. **Failed to correct the error when notified** — Despite Centrix formally notifying them of the dispute in May 2024, Latitude has not corrected the error.

3. **Failed to respond to Centrix** — Latitude repeatedly failed to respond to Centrix's requests, in apparent breach of their obligations as a credit reporter.

4. **Failed to respond to my complaints** — Latitude has not provided any substantive response to my direct complaints or escalations.

5. **Breached the Credit Reporting Privacy Code** — Under New Zealand's Credit Reporting Privacy Code, credit providers must ensure reported information is accurate and must respond to correction requests in a timely manner. Latitude has failed to do either.

6. **Provided poor customer service** — The buck-passing between Latitude and Centrix, combined with Latitude's failure to take ownership of the issue, represents unacceptable customer service.
