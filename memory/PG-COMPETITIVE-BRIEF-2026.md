## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Market Landscape:** NZ wealth management is consolidating (mergers, acquisitions) while simultaneously fragmenting (digital disruptors, niche players). Traditional firms (Public Trust, JBWere) rely on heritage + referrals. Modern players (Forsyth Barr) invest in digital content. Disruptors (Conveyancing Shop) compete on transparency + UX.

**PG's Position:** Mid-transition from "trustee services heritage brand" → "full-spectrum wealth management powerhouse." Has the assets ($2.5B AUM) and ambition (2 acquisitions in 18 months) but lacks the **digital acquisition infrastructure** to scale retail.

**Strategic Opportunity:** First-mover advantage in **digital-first, transparent, cross-sell integrated** wealth marketing. Competitors are either asleep (Public Trust, JBWere) or narrowly focused (Forsyth Barr = investments only, Conveyancing Shop = legal only). PG can own the **full wealth continuum** (estate planning → investments → philanthropy) if they move fast.

## DEEP DIVE: KEY COMPETITORS

### 1. Public Trust (Govt-Backed Incumbent)

**Strengths:**
- 150+ years heritage
- Quasi-official status (trusted for wills, estates)
- Nationwide physical presence
- Strong 60+ demographic penetration

**Weaknesses (PG's Opportunities):**
- **Aging brand perception:** Not cool, not modern, not appealing to 35-50 demo
- **Digital immaturity:** Website is transactional, not educational; SEO is thin
- **Slow innovation:** Government legacy = bureaucratic, risk-averse
- **No investment management integration:** Wills/trusts only, not wealth management

**PG's Attack Strategy:**
- **Own the 35-50 demographic:** Target life-event triggers (marriage, kids, home purchase) that Public Trust ignores
- **Digital dominance:** Outrank them on Google for "will NZ," "estate planning Auckland," etc.
- **Modern brand refresh:** Position PG as "heritage you can trust + innovation you deserve"
- **Cross-sell advantage:** Will → Trust → Investments (Public Trust can't compete here)

**Marketing Tactics:**
- Google Ads targeting "Public Trust alternatives" (competitor conquest)
- Content: "Why modern families choose Perpetual Guardian over Public Trust"
- Transparency: Publish will pricing publicly (Public Trust doesn't)

---

### 2. Forsyth Barr (Digital-Savvy Investment Firm)

**Strengths:**
- 85+ years, staff-owned (alignment narrative)
- 25 offices nationwide
- **Strong content marketing:** Webinars, market insights, research reports
- **Her Wealth programme:** Niche targeting (women investors) = smart
- Award-winning research division

**Weaknesses (PG's Opportunities):**
- **Investment-only focus:** No estate planning integration (missed cross-sell)
- **Higher net-worth skew:** Not targeting $50k-$500k retail investors aggressively
- **No digital direct-to-consumer platform:** Still adviser-dependent

**PG's Attack Strategy:**
- **Match their content output, 2x the distribution:** If Forsyth Barr does 1 webinar/month, PG does 2
- **Launch niche targeting beyond gender:** "Wealth for Business Owners," "Iwi Investment Strategies," "Farming Family Succession"
- **Cross-sell integration:** "We don't just manage your investments—we plan your estate, structure your trusts, and guide your philanthropy. Forsyth Barr can't."
- **DTC platform:** Beat them to market with a seamless digital onboarding experience

**Marketing Tactics:**
- Content series: "What Forsyth Barr won't tell you about estate planning" (positioning PG as holistic vs. narrow)
- Her Wealth competitor: Launch "Business Wealth" or "Farming Wealth" (niche targeting)
- Webinar funnel: Monthly educational events → lead capture → nurture → conversion

---

### 3. JBWere (Ultra-High-Net-Worth Specialist)

**Strengths:**
- Institutional credibility (ANZ-owned)
- Ultra-wealthy client roster (iwi, private companies, family offices)
- "Safeguard New Zealand's wealth" brand positioning

**Weaknesses (PG's Opportunities):**
- **Exclusive positioning = limited TAM:** They ignore $1M-$10M families (PG's sweet spot)
- **Minimal public marketing:** Relationship-driven, not scalable
- **No retail offering:** Not interested in $50k-$500k investors

**PG's Attack Strategy:**
- **"Accessible JBWere":** Position PG as "institutional-quality wealth management for families with $1M-$10M, not just ultra-wealthy"
- **Leverage Tim Chesterfield's pedigree:** 35 years, QBE Insurance, US$1B+ portfolio management = institutional credibility without the exclusivity
- **Target JBWere's "too small" clients:** Business owners, successful professionals, farming families who don't meet JBWere's $10M+ threshold

**Marketing Tactics:**
- Content: "You don't need $50M to deserve institutional-quality wealth management"
- Case studies: "How we helped a $3M family achieve their wealth goals" (relatable, not intimidating)
- SEO: Own "wealth management for business owners NZ," "family office alternatives NZ"

## MARKET GAPS (PG's BLUE OCEAN OPPORTUNITIES)

### Gap 1: No One Owns "Wealth Continuum Marketing"

**Current State:**
- Public Trust = wills/trusts only
- Forsyth Barr = investments only
- JBWere = ultra-wealthy only
- Conveyancing Shop = cheap legal only

**PG's Opportunity:**
- **Own the narrative:** "Your wealth journey shouldn't be fragmented. One partner for estate planning, investment management, and philanthropy."
- **Marketing message:** "From your first will to your family's philanthropic legacy—we're with you every step."

**Tactics:**
- Content series: "The 5 Stages of Wealth" (accumulation, protection, growth, transition, legacy)
- Lifecycle marketing: Email nurture that evolves with client life stage
- Case study: "The Johnson Family: How we managed their wealth from $500k to $5M over 20 years"

---

### Gap 2: 35-50 Demographic Underserved

**Current State:**
- Everyone targets 55+ (approaching retirement/death = traditional estate planning trigger)
- 35-50 = "too young" for wills, "too busy" for investment planning (myth)

**PG's Opportunity:**
- **Target life-event triggers:** Marriage, first child, home purchase, business ownership, parents aging
- **Messaging:** "You don't wait until you're sick to get health insurance—why wait until you're old to plan your estate?"

**Tactics:**
- Facebook/Instagram ads targeting "recently engaged," "new parents," "first-time homebuyers"
- Content: "Estate planning for young families," "Investing your first $100k," "Business succession for entrepreneurs under 50"
- Partnerships: Wedding planners, mortgage brokers, business coaches (referral networks)

---

### Gap 3: Transparent Pricing

**Current State:**
- Most firms hide pricing behind "call for quote" or "contact us" (creates friction)
- Only disruptors (Conveyancing Shop) publish fixed prices

**PG's Opportunity:**
- **Be the first premium firm to publish transparent pricing**
- **Builds trust:** "We have nothing to hide"
- **Reduces friction:** Converts browsers into inquiries

**Tactics:**
- Pricing page: Clear tiers (Basic, Standard, Premium) for wills, trusts, estate plans
- Investment fees: Published management fees (e.g., 0.65%-1.4%) on website
- Comparison table: "PG vs. competitors—what you get for your money"

---

### Gap 4: Digital-First Client Journey

**Current State:**
- Most firms require phone calls → office visits → paper forms (slow, inconvenient)
- Younger demo expects digital-first (book online, video consults, e-signatures)

**PG's Opportunity:**
- **Seamless digital onboarding:** Book consultation online (Calendly) → video call (Zoom) → e-sign documents (DocuSign) → account dashboard (client portal)
- **Mobile-optimized:** Website, forms, client portal all work perfectly on phone

**Tactics:**
- Build DTC website with online booking (launching "soon"—Tom accelerates this)
- Video consultation option (not required, but available)
- Client portal: Track estate plan progress, view investment portfolio, download documents
