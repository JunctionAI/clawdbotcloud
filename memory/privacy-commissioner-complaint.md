## 1. Executive Summary

I am lodging a formal complaint regarding Latitude Financial Services' failure to correct a credit reporting error on my Centrix credit file for **nearly two years** (May 2024 – present). Despite extensive documented attempts to have this error corrected through both Centrix and Latitude directly, Latitude has repeatedly failed to respond within statutory timeframes, failed to investigate the matter properly, and failed to correct the inaccurate information.

This ongoing failure has caused material harm to my credit profile and constitutes serious breaches of the Credit Reporting Privacy Code 2020.

---

## 2. Background

In early 2024, I made a payment on my GEM Visa credit card account (administered by Latitude Financial Services). Due to what I understand was a system outage on Latitude's end, this payment was incorrectly recorded as "missed" despite being made on time. This resulted in arrears being erroneously reported to Centrix, negatively impacting my credit file.

I became aware of this error and initiated a correction request through Centrix in May 2024. What should have been a straightforward correction has instead become a nearly two-year ordeal of unanswered emails, broken commitments, and ongoing credit damage.

## 5. Breaches of the Credit Reporting Privacy Code 2020

### 5.1 Breach of Rule 6 – Accuracy

**Rule 6** requires credit reporters and credit providers to take reasonable steps to ensure that credit information is accurate, up to date, complete, and not misleading.

**Breach:** Latitude has failed to maintain accurate credit information. The reported arrears are factually incorrect – my payment was made on time but incorrectly recorded due to a system error on Latitude's end. Despite being notified of this error, Latitude has taken no steps to correct the inaccurate information.

### 5.2 Breach of Rule 7 – Correction of Credit Information

**Rule 7** establishes that when a credit provider receives a correction request (either directly or via a credit reporter), they must:
- Respond within **20 working days**
- Take reasonable steps to investigate the request
- Either correct the information or provide reasons for refusal

**Breach:** Latitude has failed to respond within statutory timeframes on multiple occasions:
- Initial request: May 2024 – no response within 20 working days
- Follow-up (September 2024) – no timely response
- Follow-up (November 2024) – no timely response
- Urgent escalation (April 2025) – no timely response
- Final notice (June 2025) – only automated response received

Centrix has documented their own difficulties obtaining responses from Latitude, with Viviane explicitly noting on multiple occasions that "Latitude does take a long time to respond."

### 5.3 Breach of Rule 10 – Rights of Access and Correction

**Rule 10** reinforces individuals' rights under the Privacy Act 2020 to have their credit information corrected.

**Breach:** By failing to engage with correction requests for nearly two years, Latitude has effectively denied me my statutory right to have inaccurate personal information corrected.

### 5.4 Failure to Act in Good Faith

Throughout this process, Latitude has:
- Directed me to contact Centrix when I contacted them directly
- Failed to respond to Centrix's requests on my behalf
- Sent only automated replies despite "URGENT" and "final notice" markers
- Made no apparent effort to investigate or resolve the error

This pattern of conduct suggests a systemic failure to handle correction requests appropriately, or worse, deliberate disregard for statutory obligations.

## 7. Resolution Sought

I am seeking the following resolution:

### 7.1 Immediate Correction
Latitude must immediately correct the inaccurate arrears information reported to Centrix and any other credit reporting agencies. The correction should:
- Remove all record of the incorrectly reported missed payment/arrears
- Confirm the correction in writing to me
- Provide confirmation that Centrix and any other affected credit bureaus have been notified

### 7.2 Written Apology
A formal written apology from Latitude acknowledging:
- The error in credit reporting
- Their failure to respond to correction requests within statutory timeframes
- The length of time this matter has remained unresolved

### 7.3 Compensation
Given the extended duration of this matter (nearly 2 years), the multiple breaches of the Credit Reporting Privacy Code 2020, and the harm suffered, I am seeking compensation for:

- **Credit damage:** Reputational harm from 21+ months of incorrect credit reporting
- **Time and effort:** Extensive time spent pursuing this matter
- **Emotional distress:** Stress and anxiety caused by Latitude's conduct

I believe compensation in the range of **$3,000 - $5,000** would be appropriate given the seriousness and duration of the breaches.

### 7.4 Systemic Review
I request that the Privacy Commissioner investigate whether Latitude has systemic issues with handling credit correction requests, given:
- Centrix's repeated documentation of Latitude's slow responses
- The pattern of non-engagement despite escalation
- The possibility that other consumers have been similarly affected
