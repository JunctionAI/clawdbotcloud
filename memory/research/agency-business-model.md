## Part 1: Pricing Models for Premium Agencies

### Industry Benchmarks for Premium Agency Pricing

| Tier | Monthly Retainer | Annual Value | Typical Client Size |
|------|------------------|--------------|---------------------|
| Mid-Market | $10k-$25k | $120k-$300k | $10M-$50M revenue |
| Enterprise | $25k-$75k | $300k-$900k | $50M-$500M revenue |
| Strategic Partner | $75k-$200k | $900k-$2.4M | $500M+ revenue |

### Pricing Philosophy for AI Services

**Key Insight:** Don't price AI services by the hour or by AI costs. Price by **value delivered** and **capability access**.

**Three Pricing Approaches:**

#### 1. Value-Based Pricing (Recommended Primary Model)
- Price based on business outcomes: leads generated, revenue influenced, efficiency gained
- Example: If your AI content engine generates $2M in attributed pipeline, charge $200k-$400k (10-20%)
- Requires robust attribution and reporting
- Best for performance marketing, lead gen, content-to-conversion work

#### 2. Capability Access Pricing
- Monthly fee for access to your AI workforce + expert management
- Like an "AI department as a service"
- Predictable revenue, easier to sell
- Example: $35k/month for a full AI content + distribution operation

#### 3. Hybrid: Base + Performance
- Base retainer covers AI infrastructure + management
- Performance bonus tied to KPIs
- Example: $20k/month base + 5% of attributed revenue above baseline
- Aligns incentives, reduces client risk perception

### Recommended Structure: **Retainer-First with Project On-Ramps**

**Strategy:**
1. **Entry Point:** Offer "AI Readiness Assessments" or "AI Marketing Audits" as $15k-$30k projects
2. **Conversion:** Assessment identifies opportunities → propose retainer engagement
3. **Lock-In:** 12-month minimum contracts with 90-day notice for cancellation
4. **Expansion:** Quarterly business reviews identify expansion opportunities

### Retainer Structure Options

#### Option A: Fixed Monthly Retainer
```
$35,000/month
- Includes: 3 AI agents, 40 hours expert oversight, unlimited content
- 12-month commitment, paid quarterly in advance
- 10% discount for annual prepayment
```

#### Option B: Tiered Usage Retainer
```
Base: $20,000/month (platform access + strategy)
Usage: 
  - AI compute: $2-5k/month (passed through at cost + 20%)
  - Expert hours: $250/hour over 20 included hours
  - Additional agents: $3k/month each
```

#### Option C: Outcome-Based Retainer
```
Base: $15,000/month (minimum commitment)
Performance: +$X per qualified lead, +Y% of attributed revenue
Cap: $75,000/month maximum
```

### Contract Terms (Premium Standard)

- **Minimum Term:** 12 months
- **Payment:** Net-15, quarterly in advance preferred
- **Scope Creep Protection:** Clearly defined deliverables, change order process
- **Cancellation:** 90-day written notice, fees continue during notice period
- **Rate Increases:** Annual increases of 3-5% built into contract

## Part 4: Margin Analysis - AI vs. Human Costs

### The AI Advantage: Unit Economics

#### Traditional Agency Model (Human-Only)
```
Senior Strategist: $150,000/year fully loaded
  → Billable hours: 1,400/year (70% utilization)
  → Effective cost: $107/billable hour
  → Bill rate: $250-$350/hour
  → Margin per hour: $143-$243 (57-69%)

Content Writer: $80,000/year fully loaded
  → Output: ~100 blog posts/year (quality work)
  → Cost per post: $800
  → Bill rate: $1,500-$2,500/post
  → Margin per post: $700-$1,700 (47-68%)
```

#### AI-Augmented Model (JunctionMedia.ai)
```
AI Content Agent: ~$500/month compute + tools
  → Output: 200+ blog posts/month (with human QA)
  → Cost per post: $2.50 (AI only)
  → Add human QA (5 min/post × $50/hr): $4.17
  → Total cost per post: $6.67
  → Bill rate: $500-$1,000/post
  → Margin per post: $493-$993 (99-99%)

Expert Oversight: $200,000/year fully loaded
  → Can manage 5-10x more output than traditional model
  → Effective cost spread across much larger revenue base
```

## Appendix A: Sales Playbook Summary

### Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

- **Company Size:** $10M-$500M revenue
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS, Professional Services, E-commerce, FinTech
- **Marketing Team:** 2-10 people (big enough to need help, small enough to want AI leverage)
- **Pain Points:** 
  - "We can't produce enough content"
  - "Our team is stretched too thin"
  - "We want to use AI but don't know how"
  - "Agency costs are too high for what we get"

### Qualification Questions

1. What's your current marketing team structure?
2. How much content do you produce monthly? How much do you need?
3. Have you explored AI tools? What was your experience?
4. What would 10x content output mean for your business?
5. What's your current agency spend? What's the ROI?

### Objection Handling

**"It's too expensive"**
→ "Let's compare: A content team of 3 costs $300k+/year. We deliver more output for less, with expert strategy included. What's your current content cost per piece?"

**"We can do AI ourselves"**
→ "You absolutely can. Most companies find the challenge isn't the AI—it's the prompt engineering, quality control, strategy, and constant optimization. We've done this 100+ times. How much is your team's time worth?"

**"What if AI quality isn't good enough?"**
→ "Great question. Every piece goes through expert review. We guarantee quality—and we've built proprietary workflows that produce consistently better output than generic AI tools."
