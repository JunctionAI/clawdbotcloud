**Date:** 2026-02-06

## Executive Summary

The AI marketing agency landscape is rapidly evolving with distinct categories emerging. Most competitors fall into one of four buckets: AI SaaS tools, automation platforms, enterprise giants, or traditional agencies adding AI. **The critical gap is in full-service AI agency delivery** - where human strategy meets AI execution at SME-accessible price points.

**Key Opportunity:** No one is truly offering "AI agents doing the actual work" as a managed service for small-medium businesses. Everyone is either selling tools (DIY) or enterprise solutions ($50k+/month).

## 4. What Competitors Are Doing Wrong

### Problem 1: DIY Overload
Most AI tools require customers to become operators. Jasper, Copy.ai, Relevance AI - all need someone to build, manage, iterate. **SMEs don't have that capacity.**

### Problem 2: Enterprise Lock-Out
Salesforce Agentforce, HubSpot Breeze, UiPath - all require existing platform commitment or enterprise budgets. **The missing middle is unserved.**

### Problem 3: AI as Feature, Not Foundation
Traditional agencies adding AI still charge for human hours. They're using GPT to speed up writing but not reimagining the delivery model. **Cost structure unchanged.**

### Problem 4: Platform Dependency
Many solutions require buying into ecosystems (Salesforce, HubSpot, Meta). **Platform-agnostic solutions are rare.**

### Problem 5: No Accountability
SaaS platforms provide tools, not outcomes. "Here's an AI agent" vs "Here's 50 qualified leads." **Outcome-based AI services don't exist.**

### Problem 6: Technical Barrier
CrewAI, n8n, even Relevance AI require technical skills to implement properly. **Business owners can't self-serve.**

### Problem 7: Generic, Not Industry-Specific
Most AI tools are horizontal. No deep expertise in specific verticals. **Niche specialization is missing.**
