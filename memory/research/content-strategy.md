## Executive Summary

This strategy positions Tom as a trusted AI authority who attracts C-suite buyers through demonstrated expertise rather than outbound sales tactics. The core philosophy: **be so valuable in public that people seek you out privately.**

## Part 1: LinkedIn Thought Leadership (Primary Channel)

### Why LinkedIn First
- 4 of 5 B2B leads from social come from LinkedIn
- C-suite executives scroll LinkedIn more than any other platform
- Algorithm rewards expertise and engagement, not sales pitches
- Long-form content performs exceptionally well

### Content Pillars (Rotate Weekly)

**Pillar 1: AI Reality Check**
- Myth-busting AI hype
- "What AI actually can/can't do for your business"
- Cut through vendor BS with honest takes

**Pillar 2: Strategic Insights**
- How AI fits into business strategy (not just tech)
- ROI frameworks for AI investment
- Decision-making guides for executives

**Pillar 3: Behind the Build**
- Real project stories (anonymized if needed)
- Challenges faced and solved
- Technical decisions explained simply

**Pillar 4: Industry POV**
- Hot takes on AI news
- Predictions (with reasoning)
- What competitors won't tell you

### Posting Cadence
- **Daily:** 1 post (mix of pillars)
- **Twice weekly:** Engage meaningfully on 10-20 relevant posts
- **Weekly:** 1 long-form article or document

### LinkedIn Post Formats That Work

**1. The Contrarian Take**
```

They're wrong.

Here's what actually happens when companies [do thing]:
[3-5 bullet points of reality]

```

**2. The Breakdown**
```

Here's what I learned:

1. [Insight]

The biggest surprise? [Hook]
```

**3. The Story**

**4. The List**
```

...

```

## Part 2: Case Studies That Convert

### The High-Converting Case Study Framework

**Structure: Problem → Process → Proof**

#### Section 1: The Situation (20%)
- Client context (industry, size, challenge)
- The pain point in their words
- What they'd already tried
- Stakes if nothing changed

#### Section 2: The Journey (40%)
- Discovery process (how you understood the real problem)
- Strategic approach (the thinking, not just the doing)
- Key decisions and why
- Challenges overcome
- Timeline and milestones

#### Section 3: The Results (40%)
- Quantified outcomes (specific numbers)
- Before/after comparison
- Client quote (emotional + logical)
- Unexpected benefits
- What's next for them

### Case Study Formats

**1. The One-Pager** (For busy executives)
- Single page PDF
- Hero stat at top
- 3 bullet problem, 3 bullet solution, 3 bullet results
- One strong client quote
- Clear CTA

**2. The Deep Dive** (For technical buyers)
- 4-6 pages
- Full narrative with technical details
- Architecture decisions explained
- Lessons learned section
- Detailed metrics

**3. The Video Case Study** (For social/web)
- 2-3 minutes
- Client speaking (with B-roll)
- Metric overlays
- Emotional + logical appeal

**4. The Carousel** (For LinkedIn)
- 8-10 slides
- One idea per slide
- Strong hook on slide 1
- Results on final slides
- Comment to get full case study

### What Makes Case Studies Convert

**DO:**
- Lead with the transformation, not the tech
- Use specific numbers ("43% reduction" not "significant improvement")
- Include the client's voice
- Show your thinking process
- Address objections implicitly through the narrative
- End with a soft CTA

**DON'T:**
- Make it about you (make it about them)
- Use jargon the reader won't understand
- Hide failures or challenges
- Exaggerate results
- Forget the emotional component

### Case Study Promotion Strategy
1. Announce on LinkedIn with key insight (not just "new case study")
2. Break into 3-4 standalone posts over 2 weeks
3. Reference in relevant conversations
4. Include in email sequences
5. Use in proposals and sales conversations
6. Update annually with long-term results

## Part 3: Email Marketing for Agency Lead Gen

### The "Attract" Email Philosophy
- No cold pitching
- Value first, always
- Build relationship over time
- Let them come to you

### Lead Magnet Ideas

**High-Value Downloads:**
1. "The AI Readiness Assessment" (self-scoring checklist)
2. "5 AI Use Cases by Industry" (tailored PDFs)
3. "The Executive's Guide to AI Vendor Selection"
4. "AI Project Budget Calculator" (interactive)
5. "The AI Pilot Playbook" (step-by-step guide)

### Email Sequence: New Subscriber (7 emails over 21 days)

**Email 1 (Day 0): Welcome + Deliver**
- Deliver the lead magnet
- Set expectations
- One personal story
- No pitch

**Email 2 (Day 3): Quick Win**
- One actionable tip they can use today
- Show expertise through helpfulness
- Invite reply with questions

**Email 3 (Day 7): The Story**
- Client transformation story
- Emotional + logical
- Link to full case study (optional)

**Email 4 (Day 10): The Myth Buster**
- Challenge common assumption
- Provide alternative perspective
- Position as trusted advisor

**Email 5 (Day 14): Behind the Scenes**
- How you work
- Your values/approach
- Build personal connection

**Email 6 (Day 18): The Resource**
- Curated tools/resources
- Show generosity
- No strings attached

**Email 7 (Day 21): The Soft Offer**
- "If you're thinking about AI..."
- Calendar link for exploration call
- No pressure, no urgency hacks
- Clear value proposition

### Ongoing Newsletter Strategy

**Frequency:** Bi-weekly or monthly (consistency > frequency)

**Format:**
1. **One Big Idea** (300-500 words of insight)
2. **What I'm Seeing** (trends, observations)
3. **Resource of the Week** (tool, article, framework)
4. **Optional: Current availability** (only if genuine)

**Subject Line Patterns:**
- Questions: "Is your AI strategy actually a strategy?"
- Specificity: "The 23-minute AI audit"
- Curiosity: "What I learned from a failed AI project"
- Directness: "AI reality check: March 2025"

### Email Don'ts
- No countdown timers
- No fake scarcity
- No "I noticed you haven't..."
- No guilt trips
- No hard sells
- No buying email lists

## Part 4: Video Content Strategy

### Video Hierarchy (Start Simple, Scale Up)

**Level 1: LinkedIn Native Video (Start Here)**
- 60-90 seconds
- Phone camera is fine
- One insight per video
- Vertical format
- Captions always

**Level 2: YouTube Long-Form (Build To)**
- 8-15 minutes
- Tutorial/explainer style
- Evergreen content
- Better production value
- SEO-focused titles

**Level 3: Podcast/Interview Guest (Opportunistic)**
- Position as expert
- Borrowed audience
- Relationship building
- Content repurposing gold

### Video Content Ideas

**Quick Takes (LinkedIn):**
1. "One AI myth busted in 60 seconds"
2. "The question every CEO should ask about AI"
3. "This week in AI: What it means for you"
4. "Behind the scenes: [Current project]"
5. "The tool I can't stop recommending"
6. "Reacting to [AI news/announcement]"

**Deep Dives (YouTube):**
1. "How to evaluate an AI vendor (complete guide)"
2. "AI use case breakdown: [Industry]"
3. "Building vs buying AI solutions: Decision framework"
4. "AI project post-mortem: What went wrong"
5. "The AI stack I recommend in 2025"
6. "Live: Building [something] with AI"

### Production Tips (Premium Feel, Minimal Budget)
- Good lighting > good camera (ring light or window)
- Good audio > good video (lapel mic or podcast mic)
- Clean, branded background
- Look at camera, not screen
- Edit ruthlessly (cut the fluff)
- Add captions (80% watch muted)
- Strong thumbnail with text

### Repurposing System
1. Record 15-min video
2. Extract 3-4 short clips for LinkedIn
3. Transcribe for blog post
4. Pull quotes for social posts
5. Use audio for podcast if applicable
6. Screenshots for carousel

## Part 5: PR and Media Opportunities

### Building Media Presence

**Tier 1: Industry Publications**
- Target: TechCrunch, Wired, VentureBeat, AI-specific pubs
- Approach: Contributed articles, expert commentary
- How: Build relationships with journalists covering AI

**Tier 2: Business Press**
- Target: Forbes, Inc, Entrepreneur, Fast Company
- Approach: Founder stories, business insights
- How: Contributor programs, HARO responses

**Tier 3: Local/Regional**
- Target: NZ Herald, Stuff, NBR, local business press
- Approach: "Local founder" angle, NZ AI landscape
- How: Direct outreach, press releases for milestones

**Tier 4: Podcasts**
- Target: AI podcasts, business podcasts, startup podcasts
- Approach: Guest expert
- How: Direct pitch with angle

### HARO/Qwoted Strategy
- Sign up for journalist query services
- Respond to AI, tech, business queries
- Keep responses tight and quotable
- Build byline portfolio over time

### Angles That Get Coverage

**Trend Commentary:**
- "NZ AI adoption is 18 months behind Australia. Here's why that's changing."
- "Why [major AI release] matters for SMBs"
- "The real state of AI in [industry]"

**Contrarian Takes:**
- "Most AI startups will fail—and that's good for business"
- "The AI skills gap is a myth"
- "Why I tell clients NOT to use AI (sometimes)"

**Data/Research:**
- Original surveys or analysis
- "We analyzed 50 AI projects. Here's what worked."
- Benchmark reports

**Human Interest:**
- Founder journey
- Client transformation stories
- Team culture/approach

### Building Journalist Relationships
1. Follow AI/tech journalists on Twitter
2. Engage with their work (thoughtfully)
3. Share their articles with genuine commentary
4. Offer to be a source (not a story)
5. Make their job easier (provide data, quotes, angles)
6. Never pitch blind—build relationship first

### Speaking Opportunities
- Local tech meetups (start small)
- Industry conferences (AI, digital, business)
- Webinars (host or guest)
- Podcast guesting
- LinkedIn Live sessions

## Part 6: What NOT to Do (Avoid Looking Desperate)

### The Desperation Signals

**On LinkedIn:**
❌ "Looking for new clients!" posts
❌ Commenting "DM me!" on every relevant post
❌ Automated connection requests with pitch
❌ Celebrating every small win performatively
❌ Posting about being "open for projects"
❌ Engagement pods and fake comments
❌ Tagging people hoping they'll share

**In Email:**
❌ "Just following up" sequences (5+ emails)
❌ Fake personalization ("I loved your company!")
❌ Countdown timers and fake urgency
❌ "Spots filling up fast!" when they're not
❌ Buying lists and cold blasting
❌ Immediate hard sell after opt-in

**In Sales:**
❌ Dropping price unprompted
❌ "I'll do anything to win this"
❌ Chasing with multiple follow-ups
❌ Badmouthing competitors
❌ Overselling capabilities
❌ Taking any client at any price

**In Content:**
❌ Obvious ChatGPT-written posts
❌ Engagement bait with no substance
❌ Posting 10x a day for algorithm
❌ Copying competitors' content style
❌ All promotion, no value
❌ Inconsistent then burst posting

### The Confidence Signals (Do This Instead)

**Abundance Mindset:**
✅ "We work with clients who [specific criteria]"
✅ "This isn't right for everyone, and that's okay"
✅ "We're selective about projects we take on"
✅ "Our process starts with fit assessment"

**Value Demonstration:**
✅ Give away your best insights freely
✅ Help people even when they can't pay
✅ Share what you're learning, not what you're selling
✅ Be genuinely useful in comments

**Patient Positioning:**
✅ Play the long game (12+ months)
✅ Build relationships before you need them
✅ Stay consistent even when nothing's happening
✅ Trust the compound effect

### The "Would a Premium Brand Do This?" Test

Before any marketing action, ask:
> "Would McKinsey/Apple/[respected brand] do this?"

If the answer is no, don't do it.

## Part 8: Measuring Success

### Vanity vs. Real Metrics

**Track but don't obsess:**
- Follower count
- Post impressions
- Likes/comments

**Actually matters:**
- Inbound inquiry volume
- Quality of inquiries
- DM conversations started
- Email list growth
- Newsletter open/click rates
- Time on site (from social)
- Proposal requests

### 90-Day Targets (Realistic)

**LinkedIn:**
- +500-1000 followers (quality over quantity)
- 5-10 meaningful DM conversations/month
- 2-3 inbound inquiries/month

**Email:**
- 100-300 subscribers
- 40%+ open rate
- 5-10% click rate

**Content:**
- 60+ pieces published
- 1-2 viral (relative) posts
- Consistent posting streak

**Business:**
- 2-3 qualified leads from content
- 1 client from content (stretch)
- Clear voice/positioning established

### Quarterly Review Questions
1. What content performed best? Why?
2. Where are leads actually coming from?
3. What feels authentic vs. forced?
4. What should I do more/less of?
5. Am I attracting the right people?

## Final Philosophy

**The "attract don't chase" approach works because:**
1. Premium clients want to discover you, not be sold to
2. Expertise demonstrated is more powerful than expertise claimed
3. The internet rewards consistent value creation
4. Trust builds slowly but converts quickly
5. One great client from content > 100 cold emails

**The timeline reality:**
- Month 1-3: Building foundation (feels slow)
- Month 3-6: Gaining momentum (starts working)
- Month 6-12: Compounding effect (leads arrive)
- Year 2+: Inbound machine (clients find you)

**Play the long game. Be patient. Be valuable. They'll come.**
