# Subject Line Formulas

## High-Converting Formulas

### Curiosity Gap
Create intrigue without being clickbait.

- "What most supplement companies won't tell you"
- "The real reason NZ mussels are different"
- "We need to talk about [topic]"

### Direct Benefit
Clear, specific value proposition.

- "Wake up with more energy"
- "Joint support that actually works"
- "Better sleep starts tonight"

### Personal & Conversational
Feels like it's from a friend.

- "Quick question, {{ first_name }}"
- "Thought you'd want to know..."
- "This one's for you"

### Urgency (Use Sparingly)
Real deadlines only. Don't fake urgency.

- "Ends tonight at midnight"
- "Last chance: [offer]"
- "Only 24 hours left"

### Social Proof
Leverage what others are doing.

- "Our customers' #1 pick"
- "Why 10,000+ Kiwis trust this"
- "The product everyone's asking about"

### Question
Engage by asking something relevant.

- "Tired of feeling tired?"
- "What's really in your supplements?"
- "Ever wondered why...?"

### News / Announcement
Something genuinely new.

- "Introducing [product name]"
- "We've got news"
- "Something new is here"

## Preview Text Guidelines

Preview text = the second line visible in inbox. Use it to:

1. **Complement, don't repeat** the subject line
2. **Add context** or a secondary hook
3. **Create a complete thought** with subject

### Examples

| Subject | Preview Text |
|---------|--------------|
| "This weekend only: 20% off" | "Plus free shipping on orders over $75" |
| "You left something behind" | "Your Green Lipped Mussel is waiting" |
| "What most brands won't tell you" | "We believe in full transparency" |
| "Quick question" | "It'll only take a minute" |

## Testing Framework

Always A/B test subject lines. Test one variable at a time:

### Variables to Test
- **Length:** Short (under 30 chars) vs. longer
- **Emoji:** With vs. without
- **Personalisation:** Name vs. no name
- **Tone:** Urgent vs. casual
- **Format:** Question vs. statement

### Winning Patterns for DBH
Based on typical supplements/health audience:
- Personalisation improves opens by 10-20%
- Emojis: Use sparingly, test each one
- Shorter often wins on mobile
- Questions outperform statements for educational content
- Specificity beats vagueness ("Joint support" > "Feel better")

---

## What to Avoid

❌ ALL CAPS (looks like spam)
❌ Excessive punctuation!!!
❌ Clickbait that doesn't deliver
❌ "RE:" or "FWD:" tricks
❌ Spam trigger words in excess: "FREE!!!", "Act now!!!", "Limited time!!!"
❌ Misleading subject lines
❌ Emojis at the start (can look spammy)

---

## Character Limits

- **Subject line:** 30-50 characters ideal (shows fully on mobile)
- **Preview text:** 40-90 characters
- **Total visible:** ~100 characters on most clients

### Mobile Truncation
- iPhone: ~35-38 characters for subject
- Android: ~33-43 characters
- Gmail: ~70 characters (desktop)
