## 💰 WHERE THE $6.2B GAP COMES FROM

### **Trusts Under Administration**
**Likely the biggest chunk**

When PG acts as trustee, they administer trust assets including:
- Cash holdings
- Property
- Investments (may not all be in PG Funds)
- Business interests
- Other assets

**These are "assets under management" but NOT "funds under management"**

### **Estate Assets Under Administration**
When acting as executor:
- Estate assets during settlement
- Assets held temporarily
- Property sales in progress

### **Philanthropic Funds**
- Charitable trust assets
- Foundation endowments
- Grant pools
- Donor funds

### **Client Investments Held Elsewhere**
- Clients who use PG for:
  - Trustee services
  - Estate planning
  - Financial advice
- But invest through OTHER platforms (not PG Funds)
- PG still provides oversight/advice

## 🎯 Business Model Insights

### Revenue Streams (Inferred)
1. **Trust fees** - Annual trustee fees (% of trust assets)
2. **Estate administration** - Fees for executor services
3. **Legal/planning fees** - Wills, EPAs, trust setup
4. **Investment management fees** - From $2.8B FUM
5. **Financial advice fees** - Advisory services
6. **Philanthropic services** - Foundation management fees

### Cross-Sell Model
Client journey likely looks like:
1. Come for **Estate Planning** (Wills/Trusts)
2. PG becomes **Trustee** (ongoing fees on trust assets)
3. Client needs **Investment Management** → PG Funds
4. Client wants **Philanthropy** → Foundation services
5. Client needs **Financial Advice** → Advisory team

**This is why they're a "Financial Advice Provider"**
