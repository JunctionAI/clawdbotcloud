# Discovery Call Script - Clawdbot Setup Services

**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Goal:** Qualify, assess fit, recommend tier, book next step  
**Tone:** Consultative, not salesy

---

## Pre-Call Preparation (5 min before call)

- [ ] Review prospect's LinkedIn/website
- [ ] Note their industry, role, company size
- [ ] Check if they replied to outreach (read their response)
- [ ] Have Calendly open (for scheduling follow-up)
- [ ] Have proposal template ready (to customize post-call)

---

## Call Structure

### Minutes 0-5: Rapport & Context

**Opening:**
> "Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on! Before we dive in, tell me a bit about [Company]—what do you do, and what's your role there?"

**Listen for:**
- Industry
- Company size (solo vs team)
- Their specific role (founder, exec, consultant, etc.)

**Transition:**
> "Got it. So what prompted you to take this call? What's the biggest time-waster for you right now?"

---

### Minutes 5-15: Needs Assessment (The 7 Critical Questions)

**Question 1: Time Audit**
> "How many hours per week would you say you spend on email, scheduling, and admin work?"

**Listen for:**
- <5 hours = May not be a good fit (say so if true)
- 5-10 hours = Starter tier
- 10-20 hours = Professional tier
- 20+ hours (team-wide) = Enterprise tier

**Follow-up:**
> "What would you do with those hours if you got them back?"

*(Emotional hook: focus on opportunity, not just time)*

---

**Question 2: Pain Points**
> "Walk me through a typical day. What's the first thing you do when you sit down at your computer?"

**Listen for:**
- Email overwhelm ("I spend 2 hours clearing my inbox")
- Calendar chaos ("My calendar is a mess, always rescheduling")
- Research tasks ("I spend hours researching for [projects/deals/clients]")
- Admin busywork ("So much admin I can't focus on real work")

**Probe deeper:**
> "How much of that is high-value work vs busywork?"

---

**Question 3: Current Tools**
> "What tools are you using right now? Email, calendar, CRM, project management?"

**Listen for:**
- Email: Gmail or Outlook?
- Calendar: Google Calendar or Outlook?
- Messaging: WhatsApp, Slack, Telegram?
- CRM: Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, etc.?
- Project management: Asana, Monday, ClickUp, etc.?

**Why this matters:**
- Confirms technical feasibility
- Helps recommend integrations
- Identifies Pro/Enterprise tier needs (CRM = Pro minimum)

---

**Question 4: AI Experience**
> "Have you tried any AI tools before? ChatGPT, Jasper, anything like that?"

**Listen for:**
- Level of AI literacy (newbie vs power user)
- Past frustrations ("I tried ChatGPT but it didn't help with my email")
- Expectations ("I want something that just works without me managing it")

**Address concerns:**
> "Got it. The difference with what we do is it's integrated—not just a chatbot you have to manually interact with. It reads your email, monitors your calendar, and takes action proactively."

---

**Question 5: Team Size**
> "Are you flying solo, or do you have a team?"

**Listen for:**
- Solo = Starter
- 5-20 people = Professional
- 20+ people = Enterprise

**If they have a team:**
> "Would this be just for you, or would your team use it too?"

---

**Question 6: Decision-Making**
> "Are you the decision-maker on this, or would anyone else need to be involved?"

**Listen for:**
- Solo decision = can close today
- Needs approval = longer sales cycle, might need to present to others

**If they need approval:**
> "No problem. I'll put together a proposal you can share with [decision-maker]. What would they need to see to say yes?"

---

**Question 7: Timeline**
> "If we move forward, when would you want this up and running?"

**Listen for:**
- "ASAP" = high urgency, likely to close fast
- "In a few months" = low urgency, nurture for later
- "Just exploring" = qualify hard, might not be serious

**Set expectations:**
> "Our typical setup is 48-72 hours from kickoff to fully configured. Then we do a training session, and you're live."

---

### Minutes 15-25: Solution Recommendation

**Summarize what you heard:**
> "Okay, so let me make sure I understand:
> - You're spending [X] hours per week on [pain points]
> - You use [tools]
> - You're [solo/have a team]
> - You want to get [outcome] from this
> 
> Does that sound right?"

**Recommend a tier:**

**For Starter (5-10 hours/week, solo):**
> "Based on what you've told me, I'd recommend our Starter tier. Here's what that looks like:
> 
> - We professionally set up Clawdbot for you (email, calendar, messaging)
> - Install 8 core skills for the tasks you mentioned ([list 3-4 relevant ones])
> - 1-hour training so you're productive from day one
> - Ongoing support and monthly check-ins
> 
> The investment is $1,000 setup fee (one-time) + $500/month retainer.
> 
> Let's talk ROI: You said you're spending 10 hours/week on busywork. At your rate (let's say $100/hour conservatively), that's $52,000/year in time. Our Starter tier costs $7,000/year. That's a $45,000 net value.
> 
> Does that make sense?"

**For Professional (10-20 hours/week, team or complex needs):**
> "Based on your situation, I'd actually recommend Professional tier. Here's why:
> 
> - You mentioned [pain point that requires Mission Control or Heartbeat]
> - Professional includes Mission Control—5 AI agents working in parallel. So if you need research done while it's also handling your email, it can do both at once.
> - It also includes Heartbeat—proactive monitoring 2x daily. So it checks your email and calendar every morning and evening and alerts you to important stuff.
> - Plus, we build 2 custom skills per month based on your specific workflows.
> 
> Investment: $2,000 setup + $1,000/month.
> 
> ROI: You're spending 15 hours/week on this stuff. At $150/hour, that's $117,000/year in time. Professional costs $14,000/year. Net value: $103,000.
> 
> Make sense?"

**For Enterprise (20+ hours, team-wide, compliance needs):**
> "Given your team size and [compliance/security requirements], Enterprise tier is the right fit. Here's what that includes:
> 
> - Full Mission Control (10 agents)
> - 24/7 proactive monitoring (not just 2x daily)
> - Unlimited custom skills and integrations
> - Enterprise security (SSO, audit logs, compliance-ready)
> - Team onboarding for up to 10 users
> - Dedicated support with 1-hour response SLA
> 
> Investment: $5,000 setup + $2,000/month.
> 
> ROI: With your team of [X] people, if we save even 2 hours per person per week, that's [X × 2 × 50] hours per year. At $100/hour (blended rate), that's $[calculate]/year in value. Enterprise costs $29,000/year. Net value: $[calculate].
> 
> Sound good?"

---

### Minutes 25-30: Objection Handling & Close

**Address objections proactively:**

**Objection 1: "That sounds expensive"**
> "I get it. Let me break down the ROI again:
> 
> [Recalculate with their actual numbers]
> 
> The setup fee covers 5-10 hours of custom configuration work. The monthly retainer covers hosting, API costs, and ongoing support. If you're not saving at least [X] hours per month, it's not worth it for you.
> 
> Does the math make sense when you look at it that way?"

**Objection 2: "I need to think about it"**
> "Totally fair. What specifically do you need to think about? Is it the investment, the timeline, or something else?
> 
> [Address their specific concern]
> 
> I'll send you a detailed proposal today that breaks everything down. You can review it, and we can jump on a quick follow-up call if you have questions."

**Objection 3: "I'm not sure if it'll work for my [industry/role]"**
> "Great question. We've worked with [list 2-3 similar roles/industries]. The key is whether you're spending 10+ hours/week on busywork. If you are, it works.
> 
> The setup is custom to your workflows—not one-size-fits-all. That's why we do the kickoff call after you sign: to really dive into your specific use cases.
> 
> Would it help if I sent you a case study of someone in [similar role]?"

**Objection 4: "I don't have time to set this up"**
> "That's exactly why we do it for you. Your total time commitment is:
> - 1-hour kickoff call (we gather credentials and understand workflows)
> - 1-hour training (we walk you through how to use it)
> 
> That's it. We handle everything else. 48 hours later, it's ready to use.
> 
> If you didn't have to lift a finger on setup, would that change things?"

---

**Close (if they're ready):**
> "Sounds like this could be a great fit. Want to move forward?"

**If yes:**
> "Awesome. Here's what happens next:
> 1. I'll send you a proposal and service agreement today
> 2. Once you sign, I'll invoice you for the setup fee
> 3. After payment, we'll schedule the kickoff call
> 4. 48-72 hours later, you're live
> 
> Sound good? Let's get that kickoff call on the calendar now. When works for you this week?"

**If not ready:**
> "No problem. I'll send you a detailed proposal today so you have everything in writing. Can we schedule a quick 15-minute follow-up call in [2-3 days] to go over any questions?
> 
> [Schedule follow-up in Calendly]
> 
> Cool. I'll send the proposal within the next few hours. Talk soon!"

> "Great. What questions do you have?"

**If no:**
> "No worries. The TL;DR is: [TIER] tier, $[X] setup + $[Y]/month, saves you [Z] hours/week. Want me to walk you through it quickly?"

**Address any remaining concerns, then close:**
> "Does this sound like something you want to move forward with?"

**If yes:**
> "Awesome. I'll send the service agreement and invoice. Once you sign and pay the setup fee, we'll schedule the kickoff call and get you live within 48-72 hours."

> "No problem. What would need to happen for this to be a yes?"

**[Address that concern, then:]**

> "Okay. I don't want to be pushy, but I also don't want you to miss out if this could genuinely save you 10+ hours/week. How about we check back in [timeframe]?"

## Red Flags (Disqualify or Proceed Cautiously)

**Disqualify if:**
- ❌ They're spending <5 hours/week on busywork (not enough pain)
- ❌ They say "I don't see the value" after ROI explanation
- ❌ They want free trial or "try before I buy" (service business, not SaaS)
- ❌ They're shopping around for cheapest option (not a good fit for premium service)
- ❌ They want to negotiate pricing heavily (signals they don't value outcome)

**Proceed cautiously if:**
- ⚠️ They need approval from multiple people (longer sales cycle)
- ⚠️ They say "maybe in a few months" (nurture, but don't spend too much time)
- ⚠️ They have complex compliance requirements (Enterprise only, and get legal involved)
- ⚠️ They've tried AI before and were disappointed (need to overcome skepticism)

**Greenlight if:**
- ✅ They're spending 10+ hours/week on busywork (clear pain)
- ✅ They understand ROI and nod along (they get it)
- ✅ They ask "When can we start?" (high urgency)
- ✅ They're the decision-maker (can close fast)
- ✅ They value outcomes over price (will pay for results)

## Success Metrics

**Target conversion rates:**
- Discovery call → Proposal sent: 90% (almost always send proposal)
- Proposal sent → Close: 30-50%
- Overall call → Close: 30-40%

**If conversion <30%:**
- Review call recordings (if you record)
- Identify where prospects are dropping off
- Refine script or qualification process
